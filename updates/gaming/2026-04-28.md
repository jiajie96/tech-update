# Gaming Weekly Update — April 28, 2026

## Highlights

- **Microsoft retires the "Microsoft Gaming" label** — executives Asha Sharma and Matt Booty confirmed the organizational rebrand, with immediate priorities shifting to the Project Helix launch and a clearer Game Pass strategy.
- **Xbox adjusts Game Pass pricing and content** — tier prices are dropping, but Call of Duty titles will no longer arrive on the service day one. Late April additions include Double Fine's Kiln, Don't Nod's Aphelion, and Trepang2.
- **Capcom's Pragmata hits 1 million copies in 48 hours** — a strong debut that underscores demand for new IP from established studios.
- **Unity ships Platform Toolkit and AI Gateway** — two major releases aimed at simplifying cross-platform development and enabling third-party AI agent integration inside the Unity Editor.

---

## Detailed Updates

### Xbox and Game Pass

Microsoft is moving away from the "Microsoft Gaming" branding. Asha Sharma and Matt Booty shared the news internally before it went public, framing it as part of a broader organizational streamlining. The near-term focus is twofold: ensuring Project Helix launches without friction and making the Game Pass offering easier for subscribers to understand.

On the consumer side, Xbox is lowering Game Pass tier prices — a welcome change after years of incremental increases. The trade-off is that Call of Duty games will no longer land on the service at launch. For many subscribers CoD day-one access was a headline perk, so the reaction from the community will be worth watching. Meanwhile, the late April Game Pass catalog refresh brings three notable titles: Double Fine's Kiln, Don't Nod's narrative adventure Aphelion, and the frenetic FPS Trepang2.

### Game Releases and Milestones

Capcom's long-awaited Pragmata finally shipped and moved one million units in its first two days on sale. The sci-fi action title had been in and out of development limbo since its initial reveal, making the strong opening all the more significant for Capcom's new-IP pipeline.

Clair Obscur: Expedition 33, the turn-based RPG from Sandfall Interactive, celebrated its first anniversary this week having surpassed eight million copies sold. The game has maintained a steady sales trajectory well beyond its launch window, helped by consistent post-launch content.

Ubisoft officially announced Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced, a remaster of the beloved 2013 pirate adventure. It is scheduled for July 9, 2026 on PC and consoles. Details on visual and gameplay improvements are expected in the coming weeks.

### Game Engines and Tools

Unity had a busy week. The new **Platform Toolkit** is designed to reduce the overhead of shipping to multiple platforms simultaneously — handling configuration, build profiles, and platform-specific quirks in a unified interface. Alongside it, Unity launched **AI Gateway**, providing an officially supported path for connecting third-party AI agents directly to the Unity Editor. The company also previewed **Vector AI** features, though full details are still forthcoming.

On the Unreal Engine side, Epic hosted a webinar on April 24 covering MetaHumans combined with custom clothing workflows — a practical session aimed at studios building character-heavy projects.

A GDC survey released this week reported that **78% of AAA studios** are now using AI-powered tools somewhere in their production pipelines, spanning asset generation, testing, and code assistance.

### Industry Tools

JetBrains shipped **Rider 2026.1** with expanded support for game development workflows. The update includes tighter integration with Unity and Unreal projects, improved shader editing, and performance profiling enhancements — a meaningful quality-of-life bump for studios that have standardized on Rider.

Rapid advancements in AI integrations and rendering technology continue to reshape engine choices across the industry, with studios increasingly weighing built-in AI support alongside traditional performance benchmarks when selecting their tech stack.
