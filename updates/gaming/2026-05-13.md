# Gaming Weekly Update — May 13, 2026

## Highlights

- **Subnautica 2 Enters Early Access on May 14**: One of 2026's most anticipated titles hits Early Access this week, continuing the beloved underwater survival franchise with new cooperative multiplayer features.
- **European Game Engine "Immense Engine" Challenges Unreal and Unity**: Guerrilla Games co-founder Arjan Brussee is building the Immense Engine, a fully European-hosted game engine with modular AI agents at its core, aiming to rival the industry duopoly.
- **Forza Horizon 6 Leaks Ahead of May 19 Launch**: The next entry in the Forza Horizon franchise leaked online just days before its official debut, generating significant buzz across the gaming community.

## Detailed Updates

### Subnautica 2 Early Access Launch
Subnautica 2 enters Early Access on May 14, marking one of the week's biggest gaming events. The sequel to the critically acclaimed underwater survival game introduces cooperative multiplayer for the first time, allowing players to explore alien oceans together. The Early Access launch has generated strong community excitement, with the original Subnautica remaining one of the most beloved survival games of the past decade.

### Immense Engine: Europe's Unreal Rival
Guerrilla Games co-founder Arjan Brussee is developing the Immense Engine, a fully European-hosted game engine designed to challenge the Unreal-Unity duopoly. The engine takes a distinctive AI-first approach, using modular AI agents as building blocks throughout the platform. This allows developers to replace, expand, or evolve systems as AI technology advances. The engine's European hosting addresses growing data sovereignty concerns in the game development industry.

### Forza Horizon 6 Pre-Launch Leak
Forza Horizon 6 leaked online just days ahead of its official May 19 launch, with gameplay footage and details circulating across social media. The leak has generated significant discussion about the game's new features and setting, though Microsoft has not publicly commented on the early exposure.

### Directive 8020 Launches
The latest entry in The Dark Pictures Anthology, Directive 8020, launched on May 12 from Supermassive Games. Set aboard the colony ship Cassiopeia, the cinematic sci-fi horror game features actress Lashana Lynch as astronaut Brianna Young and pits players against a shapeshifting alien threat. The game continues the studio's trademark branching narrative system with life-or-death consequences.

### AI Adoption Accelerates Across Game Development
Over one-third (36%) of game industry professionals now use generative AI tools as part of their work, according to the GDC 2026 State of the Industry report. Krafton has declared itself an AI-first company, Square Enix targets having generative AI handle 70% of QA work by 2027, and EA's CEO has described AI as core to their business strategy.

### ASUS Showcases AI Gaming at Computex 2026
ASUS and Republic of Gamers announced plans to showcase next-generation AI-powered gaming innovations at Computex 2026, celebrating 20 years of ROG. The preview includes AI-enhanced gaming hardware and software solutions that leverage on-device AI processing for performance optimization and content enhancement.

### Notable Releases This Week
Beyond the major titles, this was a busy week for releases: Outbound (May 11) offers relaxing open-world camper van exploration in an eco-friendly future; Clockwork Ambrosia launched alongside Directive 8020 on May 12 with attention-grabbing stylized world design; and Dark Dominion (May 13) appeals to strategy fans with tactical sci-fi gameplay.
