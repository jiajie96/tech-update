# Gaming Weekly Update — May 7, 2026

## Highlights

- **Unity and Epic Games Announce Historic Partnership**: The two engine rivals are working together to bring Unity games into Fortnite and add Unreal Engine support to Unity's cross-platform commerce tools.
- **Unite 2026 Unveils Platform Toolkit and Multiplayer Center**: Unity's annual conference showcased a new Platform Toolkit for simplified multi-platform releases and a built-in Multiplayer Center for cross-play in Unity 6.
- **AI Adoption Grows but Developer Skepticism Rises**: 36% of game industry professionals now use generative AI tools on the job, yet 52% believe generative AI is bad for the industry — up from 30% last year.

## Detailed Updates

### Unity and Epic Games Partnership
In a move that would have been unthinkable a few years ago, Unity and Epic Games announced a collaboration to bring Unity games into Fortnite, creating new opportunities for players and developers alike. Additionally, Unity will bring Unreal Engine support to its cross-platform commerce platform, giving Unreal developers tools for managing digital catalogs, payment providers, and web shops across PC, mobile, and web, with support expected in early 2027.

### Unite 2026: Platform Toolkit and Multiplayer Center
Unity kicked off Unite 2026 with a deliberately conservative roadmap focused on developer pain points. The most impactful announcement was the new Platform Toolkit, which simplifies multi-platform releases and enhances the native in-app purchase dashboard. The new Multiplayer Center, built directly into the Unity 6 editor, gives small teams a coherent path to cross-play functionality — addressing a capability that previously required extensive custom infrastructure.

### GDC 2026 State of the Industry Report
The Game Developers Conference released its annual State of the Game Industry report, revealing the ongoing impact of layoffs, shifting attitudes toward generative AI, and market consolidation. Over one-third of game industry professionals are now using generative AI tools as part of their work, with 30% at game studios specifically reporting AI tool usage. However, developer sentiment has shifted negative, with 52% now viewing generative AI as bad for the industry.

### Engine Landscape: Unreal Leads at AA/AAA
Unreal Engine continues to dominate among larger studios, holding 42% market share overall and particularly strong at AA (59%) and AAA (47%) levels. Unity holds 30% of the market and remains popular for mobile and indie development. Godot has carved out 11% adoption primarily among newer indie developers, though it has less traction at established studios.

### ASUS and ROG Showcase Gaming AI at Computex 2026
ASUS and Republic of Gamers announced their Computex 2026 booth showcases on May 5, spotlighting next-generation AI and gaming innovation. The exhibition will celebrate 20 years of ROG while demonstrating how AI is being integrated into gaming hardware, from GPU optimization to adaptive performance tuning.

### GameMaker Integrates AI-Assisted Workflows
GameMaker has incorporated AI coding capabilities to enable AI-assisted development workflows within the engine. The integration allows developers to use natural language to generate game logic, debug code, and prototype mechanics faster, reflecting the broader trend of AI tooling being embedded directly into game development environments.

### Verse Emerges as Key Unreal Story
The Verse programming language is positioned as potentially the most important development in the Unreal Engine 5 ecosystem for 2026. Originally designed for Fortnite Creative, Verse is expanding into a broader role as Epic builds out its vision for user-generated content and metaverse-style experiences within the Unreal ecosystem.
