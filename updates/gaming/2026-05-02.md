# Gaming Weekly Update — May 2, 2026

## Highlights

- **May 2026 Delivers a Packed Release Calendar**: Forza Horizon 6, 007: First Light, Subnautica 2 Early Access, and Paralives Early Access headline an unusually dense month of major releases across all platforms.
- **GameMaker Integrates AI-Assisted "Vibe Coding"**: GameMaker's 2026 platform update adds AI-powered development workflows, C#/JS support, and GM-CLI tools, embracing the trend of natural language game development.
- **GDC 2026 Report: Unreal Engine Leads, AI Adoption Grows Unevenly**: The annual State of the Game Industry survey shows Unreal Engine at 42% primary usage, with AI tool adoption varying dramatically between studios (30%) and publishers (58%).

## Detailed Updates

### Forza Horizon 6 Launches with Japan Setting
Forza Horizon 6 from Playground Games sets the Horizon festival in Japan — from downtown Tokyo streets to the snowy Japanese Alps. The game features the series' most dense and vertical map yet, with a massive open world and an expanded car roster. Available on Xbox and PC, it arrives on the cloud via GeForce NOW on day one, reflecting the growing importance of streaming platforms for major launches.

### 007: First Light — Bond Origin Story
007: First Light launches May 27 on PS5, Xbox Series X/S, and PC. Players take on the role of a young James Bond (around 26 years old), voiced and motion-captured by Patrick Gibson. The game represents one of the most anticipated licensed game releases of the year and a major bet on narrative-driven action from IOI Interactive.

### Subnautica 2 and Paralives Enter Early Access
Subnautica 2 enters early access this month as a survival sequel set beneath alien oceans. Meanwhile, Paralives — the indie life simulation that has drawn comparisons to The Sims — finally launches in Early Access on May 25 on PC and Mac via Steam. Both titles highlight the continued viability of early access as a development and funding model for ambitious projects.

### Nintendo Switch 2: Yoshi and the Mysterious Book
Yoshi and the Mysterious Book drops exclusively for the Nintendo Switch 2 on May 21, serving as an early showcase title for the new console. The title adds to the growing Switch 2 launch window library as Nintendo works to build momentum for its successor hardware.

### GameMaker Embraces AI Development Workflows
On April 30, GameMaker announced a major platform overhaul integrating AI-assisted coding. The GMRT update adds natural-language "vibe coding" capabilities, a command-line interface (GM-CLI), and support for C# and JavaScript alongside GML. The integration allows creators to generate and iterate on game logic using conversational prompts, lowering the barrier to entry for new developers while accelerating workflows for experienced ones.

### GDC 2026: State of the Industry
The GDC 2026 State of the Game Industry report revealed key trends. Unreal Engine leads as the primary engine at 42%, followed by Unity at 30%, with Unreal's dominance strongest at AA (59%) and AAA (47%) studios. On AI adoption, 36% of game industry professionals report using generative AI tools, but adoption is heavily skewed toward publishing and marketing roles (58%) versus studio development (30%). The report also highlighted ongoing concerns about industry layoffs and their impact on institutional knowledge.

### NVIDIA GeForce NOW Adds 16 Games in May
NVIDIA's GeForce NOW cloud gaming service added 16 new titles for May 2026, including Forza Horizon 6 and 007: First Light as day-one cloud launches. The additions include support for the new GeForce RTX 5080, offering enhanced ray tracing and DLSS performance for cloud gaming subscribers.
