# Gaming Weekly Update — May 1, 2026

## Highlights

- **Microsoft Cuts Game Pass Prices**: Ultimate Game Pass drops from $29.99 to $22.99/month and PC Game Pass from $16.49 to $13.99, signaling a renewed push for subscriber growth.
- **Diablo IV: Lord of Hatred Expansion Launches**: Blizzard's major expansion for Diablo IV arrived on April 28 with overhauled endgame systems and returning crafting mechanics.
- **Industry Paradox Deepens — $195.6B Revenue, Ongoing Layoffs**: The gaming industry posted record global sales while one in three developers lost their jobs over the past year, with EA's potential acquisition and Ubisoft's second restructuring dominating business headlines.

## Detailed Updates

### Microsoft Lowers Game Pass Subscription Prices
Microsoft announced immediate price reductions across its Game Pass tiers. Ultimate Game Pass drops from $29.99 to $22.99 per month, while PC Game Pass falls from $16.49 to $13.99. The move comes as Microsoft continues its aggressive push to grow the Game Pass subscriber base following its Activision Blizzard acquisition, and may signal competitive pressure from Sony's PlayStation Plus offerings.

### Diablo IV: Lord of Hatred Ships
Blizzard launched the Lord of Hatred expansion for Diablo IV on April 28, bringing a major endgame overhaul and the return of crafting mechanics familiar to long-time series fans. The expansion is one of the biggest releases of the week alongside Far Far West and Aphelion, a sci-fi survival title set in deep space. Other notable April 28–May 1 releases include He-Man & the Masters of the Universe: Dragon Pearl of Destruction, Inkonbini: One Store Many Stories, and Invincible Vs.

### EA Acquisition Talks Continue
Electronic Arts is reportedly set to be sold to a consortium of buyers led by Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund. This would represent one of the largest gaming industry acquisitions in history and continues a trend of sovereign wealth fund investment in the gaming sector. The deal underscores the industry's strategic importance as a media and entertainment growth vector.

### Ubisoft Announces Second Restructuring
French publisher Ubisoft announced a second restructuring that will result in hundreds of additional job cuts. The company continues to struggle with underperforming releases and rising development costs. This follows a broader industry trend where major publishers are simultaneously posting record revenues while shrinking their workforces.

### Unity AI Beta 2026 Arrives
Unity launched its AI Beta 2026, with the editor now featuring AI Assistant and Generators built directly into the workflow. CEO Matt Bromberg stated that AI-driven authoring is a major focus for 2026, with the beta enabling developers to prompt full casual games into existence using natural language. Unity's AI tools automate repetitive tasks, generate placeholder assets from text prompts, and suggest code changes based on project context.

### MTG Arena Developers Unionize
The development team for Magic: The Gathering Arena passed a supermajority vote to unionize under the Communications Workers of America (CWA), becoming the United Wizards of the Coast (UWOTC) union. This continues the growing trend of game development unionization, with workers seeking better protections amid industry-wide instability.

### PlayStation Plus May 2026 Update
Sony's May PlayStation Plus update emphasizes stronger PS5 content with reduced emphasis on PS4 titles and increased partnerships with third-party publishers. The update reflects Sony's continued strategy to drive PS5 adoption as the install base matures.

### Industry Revenue Hits $195.6B Despite Workforce Challenges
The global gaming industry reached $195.6 billion in revenue, yet the workforce paradox continues: record profits alongside record layoffs. BCG's 2026 Video Gaming Report highlights platform convergence as a key growth driver, with boundaries between console, mobile, and PC breaking down, alongside user-generated content and cloud gaming driving engagement.
