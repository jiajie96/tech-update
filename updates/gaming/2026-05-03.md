# Gaming Weekly Update — May 3, 2026

## Highlights

- **Survios lays off majority of staff, studio effectively shuttering**: The veteran VR developer behind Alien: Rogue Incursion let go of most of its team on May 1, signaling continued challenges in the VR gaming market.
- **Forza Horizon 6 launches May 19 with Japan map**: The racing franchise heads to Tokyo in what's being called "the series' best map yet."
- **Unity AI Beta 2026 launches with agentic capabilities**: Unity's updated AI assistant gains significantly improved agentic features and expanded asset generation.

## Detailed Updates

### Survios Layoffs Signal VR Market Struggles
VR-focused studio Survios laid off the bulk of its development team on May 1, 2026, with employees indicating the studio will be "essentially shuttered." Survios developed nine VR games over the past decade, including Alien: Rogue Incursion. The studio had been expanding into console and PC, bringing Alien: Rogue Incursion's "Evolved Edition" to Steam, PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and Nintendo Switch 2. The layoffs follow similar cuts at Polyarc, which was unable to secure funding after a major project cancellation — painting a grim picture for dedicated VR development studios.

### Forza Horizon 6 Headlines May Releases
Forza Horizon 6 launches May 19 with a Japan-set open world centered on Tokyo, with early reception calling it the series' best map yet. Other major May releases include 007 First Light (May 27), a prequel Bond game from the Hitman creators at IO Interactive, and Subnautica 2, currently Steam's most wishlisted game.

### Unity AI Beta 2026 Brings Agentic Capabilities
Unity launched its updated AI suite this month, led by an improved AI Assistant with significantly enhanced agentic capabilities — the assistant can now handle multi-step tasks autonomously within the editor. The beta also includes upgrades to generated asset types, along with Unity AI Gateway and Unity Vector AI. According to a recent GDC survey, 78% of AAA studios now use AI-powered tools in production.

### Unreal Engine Expands AI Workflows
Epic Games continues integrating AI into Unreal Engine workflows, bringing more AI-assisted tools directly into the editor. Unreal Engine 5.5 with Nanite and Lumen remains the current major release, with AI assistants helping developers with everything from level design to quality assurance.

### GreedFall Developer Spiders Liquidated
French studio Spiders, developer of the GreedFall series, has been liquidated and permanently closed. The closure adds to a challenging period for mid-tier studios, with Behaviour Interactive (Dead by Daylight) also confirming layoffs and Moonshot Games struggling after Wildgate's commercial performance.

### Mobile Gaming Momentum Continues
May 2026 is shaping up to be a strong month for mobile gaming, building on a stacked April. Sony's MLB The Show is expanding further into mobile with authentic baseball gameplay, while numerous new titles target the growing mobile audience.

### Industry Trend: AI Permeates the Pipeline
AI tools have become a fundamental component of game development in 2026, moving beyond marketing buzzwords into daily production use. Procedural world creation, intelligent NPCs, and automated QA are the most widely adopted applications, with studios reporting significant efficiency gains across the development pipeline.
