# Gaming Weekly Update — May 4, 2026

## Highlights

- **NVIDIA Cancels New Gaming GPUs for 2026**: For the first time in 30 years, NVIDIA will not release a new generation of GeForce GPUs, with RTX 50 Super canceled and RTX 60 series pushed to 2028 due to AI-driven memory shortages.
- **GameMaker Integrates AI-Assisted Development**: GameMaker launched its new GMRT runtime and AI-powered natural language workflows, enabling "vibe coding" for game creation.
- **Developer Sentiment on AI Turns Negative**: GDC 2026 survey shows 52% of game professionals now believe generative AI is bad for the industry, up from 30% last year.

## Detailed Updates

### NVIDIA Abandons Gamers for AI

2026 marks the first year in three decades that NVIDIA won't release a new generation of consumer GeForce graphics cards. The RTX 50 Super has been canceled, and the RTX 60 series is reportedly pushed back to 2028. The decision stems from AI-fueled DRAM shortages — NVIDIA is prioritizing its far more profitable Blackwell and Rubin AI data center chips (69% operating margins) over the consumer graphics segment (40% margins). Gaming GPU production is being cut by up to 40%. The irony is stark: gaming saved NVIDIA from bankruptcy in 1999, and AI may now be the reason the company feels it no longer needs to return the favor.

### DLSS 5 Controversy

NVIDIA unveiled DLSS 5 at GTC on March 16, 2026, triggering backlash from gamers who said the new Deep Learning Super Sampling technology uses generative AI to alter the art of popular video game characters. Players describe the generative AI integration as changing artist intent, with the technology generating new visual details rather than simply upscaling existing ones. The controversy highlights growing tension between AI efficiency gains and artistic integrity in games.

### GameMaker Launches GMRT with AI Integration

GameMaker announced a major update featuring a new runtime (GMRT) designed for larger teams and broader language support, alongside AI-assisted workflows using natural language prompts. The integration allows creators to handle routine game development tasks through conversational commands, leaning into the "vibe coding" trend. GMRT represents a significant technical overhaul, moving beyond the limitations of a closed IDE to support professional studio workflows.

### GDC 2026: Developer Sentiment Shifts Against AI

The GDC 2026 State of the Game Industry report reveals that 52% of game industry professionals now believe generative AI is bad for the industry, up from 30% the previous year. Only 7% think AI is having a positive impact — a number that has steadily declined over two consecutive years. The shift reflects growing concerns about job displacement, the quality of AI-generated content, and legal uncertainties around training data.

### Engine Market Share: Unreal Leads

Current market data shows Unreal Engine at 42% adoption among developers, followed by Unity at 30%. Unreal adoption skews heavily toward larger studios — 59% of AA studios and 47% of AAA studios report it as their primary engine. The gap reflects Unreal's continued investment in high-fidelity rendering and its strong positioning for next-gen console development.

### AI in Game Development: Adoption Despite Opposition

Despite negative sentiment, the gaming industry is aggressively investing in AI to reduce costs and increase development speed. AI-driven graphics improvements, automated development pipelines, and procedural content generation are becoming standard tools. The disconnect between developer sentiment and industry investment suggests that competitive pressure is driving adoption regardless of workforce concerns.

### Industry Layoffs Continue

The GDC 2026 report also highlights the ongoing impact of layoffs across the gaming industry, with studios continuing to reduce headcount while simultaneously investing in AI tools that promise to increase per-developer output. The combination of workforce reduction and AI investment is reshaping studio structures toward smaller, more technically sophisticated teams.
