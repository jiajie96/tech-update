# Gaming Weekly Update — April 29, 2026

## Highlights

- **COCOS 4 Goes Fully Open Source Under MIT**: The COCOS game engine removed all commercial restrictions, releasing its core, cross-platform code, CLI, and IDE headless mode under the MIT license.
- **AI Dominates GDC 2026 Takeaways**: Post-GDC analysis confirms AI has moved from experimental curiosity to foundational infrastructure in game development, with tools for video generation, voice synthesis, NPC animation, and procedural content now in production.
- **Capcom's Pragmata Sells 1M Copies in Two Days**: A strong launch week for Capcom's sci-fi title demonstrates continued appetite for premium single-player experiences.

## Detailed Updates

### COCOS 4 Open Source Release
COCOS 4 is now available under the MIT license with all commercial restrictions removed. The open-source release encompasses the engine core, cross-platform runtime code, COCOS CLI, and full IDE headless mode. This is a significant move for indie and mid-tier developers who can now use a full-featured engine without licensing concerns, and it positions COCOS as a credible alternative to Godot for cross-platform 2D and mobile-first development.

### GDC 2026 Post-Mortem: AI Is Infrastructure
Analysis of GDC 2026 confirms that AI has crossed the threshold from experimental to essential. Studios demonstrated workflows where AI generates textures, environments, and animated sequences from text or sketch prompts, compressing timelines that previously required weeks. Tencent's HY 3D AI Creation Engine converts descriptions into production-quality 3D assets in minutes. NVIDIA ACE delivers production-grade voice synthesis on-device, and Ubisoft demonstrated personality-driven AI NPCs sustaining real-time conversations. However, a survey found 52% of developers still view generative AI negatively.

### Godot 4.7 Reaches Beta
Godot 4.7 entered beta, representing feature freeze with development now focused on hardening and bug fixes. The release continues Godot's steady cadence of improvements following its surge in popularity over the past two years.

### Take-Two CEO on AI and GTA 6
Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick stated that AI will assist rather than replace human creators in GTA 6's development, reflecting the industry's dominant framing of AI as a productivity tool. The comment comes as nearly all major studios now use generative AI in some capacity, according to a Google executive and industry insider.

### Capcom Pragmata Launch Success
Capcom's Pragmata sold over one million copies within its first two days on sale, demonstrating strong market performance for a new IP in a period often dominated by established franchises.

### Game Pass Late April Additions
Late April's Game Pass lineup includes Double Fine's party game Kiln, Don't Nod's atmospheric exploration adventure Aphelion, and the F.E.A.R.-inspired shooter Trepang2 — a varied selection reflecting Microsoft's continued investment in subscription content.

### Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 Anniversary
Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 celebrated its first anniversary with over eight million copies sold since launch, cementing its position as one of the breakout hits of 2025's latter half.

### Unreal Engine Indie Games Week
Epic hosted Indie Games Week on April 13 across Unreal Engine's channels, featuring exclusive developer interviews and livestreams focused on indie workflows and tips — a continued push to make Unreal more accessible to smaller teams.
