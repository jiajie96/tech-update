# Gaming Weekly Update — May 5, 2026

## Highlights

- **GameMaker integrates AI-assisted game development**: GameMaker announced AI-powered workflows enabling creators to build games using natural language prompts, leaning into the "vibe coding" trend.
- **Unity beats Q1 guidance with $505-508M revenue**: Unity's preliminary Q1 2026 results exceeded guidance of $480-490M, signaling recovery and strong developer ecosystem engagement.
- **Developer sentiment on AI sours further**: 52% of game industry professionals now believe generative AI is bad for the industry, up from 30% last year, while only 7% see positive impact.

## Detailed Updates

### GameMaker Launches AI-Assisted Development

On April 30, 2026, GameMaker announced a major platform update integrating AI-powered workflows for game creation. The integration allows creators to build games and handle routine tasks using natural language prompts. GameMaker also launched GMRT (GameMaker Runtime), a new cross-platform runtime designed to modernize its technical foundation. This positions GameMaker as one of the first mid-tier engines to fully embrace AI-assisted development.

### Unity Exceeds Q1 2026 Guidance

Unity announced preliminary Q1 2026 financial results above guidance, with revenue anticipated between $505-508 million compared to guidance of $480-490 million. Adjusted EBITDA also came in above expectations. Full results will be released May 7, 2026. The company also unveiled a new toolkit to accelerate multi-platform game launches.

### Unity-Epic Games Partnership Continues to Bear Fruit

The landmark Unity-Epic Games partnership announced at Unite in November 2025 continues to develop. Unity games can now be published into Fortnite (500M+ registered accounts), participating in the Fortnite Creator Economy. Additionally, Unity is bringing Unreal Engine support to its cross-platform commerce platform, giving Unreal developers more choice for managing digital catalogs, payment providers, and web shops.

### GDC 2026: State of the Industry

The GDC 2026 State of the Game Industry report revealed the ongoing impact of layoffs and generative AI on the sector. Key findings: Unreal Engine leads with 42% of developers using it as their primary engine, ahead of Unity at 30%. The industry continues grappling with how to integrate AI tools while addressing workforce concerns.

### Developer Backlash Against AI Intensifies

Despite industry investment in AI, developer sentiment has turned sharply negative. Digital Trends reports that 52% of game industry professionals now believe generative AI is bad for the industry (up from 30% last year), while only 7% think it's having a positive impact. Concerns center on job displacement, quality of AI-generated assets, and ethical issues around training data.

### Halo 2 and Halo 3 Remakes Announced

Microsoft confirmed that Halo 2 and Halo 3 are receiving full ground-up remakes using Unreal Engine 5 technology. This represents a significant bet on UE5 for a franchise historically built on proprietary engines, and signals broader industry confidence in Epic's technology stack.

### Meta Extends XR Commitment in Unity

Meta extended its commitment to making XR development a first-class citizen in the Unity game engine, reinforcing the platform's importance for VR/AR development despite competition from other engines and frameworks.
