# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 1, 2026

## Highlights

- **AMI Labs Raises $1.03B in Record Seed Round**: Yann LeCun's startup focused on "world models" secured the largest seed round in European history at a $3.5B valuation, backed by NVIDIA, Bezos Expeditions, and Temasek, signaling major investment in alternatives to the dominant LLM paradigm.
- **GLM-5.1 Outperforms GPT-5.4 on Coding Benchmarks**: The MIT-licensed model with 744B total parameters (40B active via MoE) beat GPT-5.4 on coding tasks and reportedly tops SWE-Bench Pro, marking a significant open-source milestone.
- **OpenAI Launches GPT-5.5**: Released on April 23, the latest iteration from OpenAI arrives as competition intensifies across the frontier model landscape.

## Detailed Updates

### AMI Labs and the Rise of World Models
Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, founded by Turing Award winner Yann LeCun after departing Meta, raised $1.03 billion in seed funding — the largest seed round in European history — at a $3.5 billion valuation. The Paris-based startup is building "world models," an alternative AI architecture to large language models that learns by understanding how the physical world works. Backers include NVIDIA, Bezos Expeditions, and Temasek, with applications targeting robotics, healthcare, and manufacturing. Nature recently profiled the broader world models trend, calling them "AI's latest sensation."

### GLM-5.1 Ships Under MIT License
Zhipu AI's GLM-5.1 has emerged as a formidable open-source contender, beating GPT-5.4 on coding benchmarks and reportedly outperforming both GPT-5.4 and Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.6 on SWE-Bench Pro. The model uses a mixture-of-experts architecture with 744B total parameters but only 40B active at inference time, making it efficient to deploy. Its MIT license makes it freely available for commercial use.

### OpenAI Releases GPT-5.5
OpenAI released GPT-5.5 on April 23, continuing its rapid release cadence. The model arrives in a crowded field: Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro and Flash-Lite have added real-time voice and image analysis, while Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.6 and Sonnet 4.6 continue to top several benchmark categories. The best-scoring models now exceed 50% accuracy on complex benchmark tests, per Stanford's 2026 AI Index.

### Google Gemma 4 Open-Source Family
Google released Gemma 4, a family of open-source multimodal models under Apache 2.0 with four variants: 27B dense, 26B-A4B MoE, and edge-optimized E2B/E4B. All variants handle text, images, and audio natively, and Google describes them as their most capable open models to date.

### Microsoft's New Foundational Models
Microsoft AI announced three new foundational models. MAI-Transcribe-1 handles speech transcription across 25 languages and runs 2.5x faster than Azure's previous Fast offering. Microsoft is positioning these models as more cost-effective alternatives to offerings from Google and OpenAI.

### OpenAI–Microsoft Exclusivity Deal Ends
Microsoft and OpenAI formally dissolved their exclusivity arrangement. Within a day, AWS rolled out three new OpenAI offerings on its Bedrock platform, including a jointly built agent service. This signals a new era of broader platform availability for OpenAI's models.

### Physics-Informed Machine Learning Advances
Researchers at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa unveiled a new algorithm advancing physics-informed machine learning, allowing AI systems to adhere to the laws of physics while processing complex datasets. The method has demonstrated improved accuracy in fluid dynamics simulations and climate modeling applications.

### Top AI Talent Exodus to Startups
A notable trend continues as senior researchers and engineers from Meta, Google, and OpenAI leave to launch AI startups. CNBC reported on April 28 that the outflow is accelerating, with investors eagerly backing teams with frontier model experience.
