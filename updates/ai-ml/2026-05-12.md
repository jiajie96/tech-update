# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 12, 2026

## Highlights

- **Anthropic Launches 10 Financial Services AI Agents**: Anthropic unveiled a suite of pre-built agent templates targeting Wall Street workflows — from pitchbook generation to KYC screening — alongside full Microsoft 365 integration and a data partnership with Moody's.
- **Pentagon Finalizes AI Contracts with Big Tech**: The U.S. Department of Defense signed AI agreements with eight major companies including OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, and NVIDIA, while notably excluding Anthropic over disagreements on use-case restrictions.
- **NVIDIA Deepens AI Investment Portfolio**: NVIDIA has committed over $40 billion in equity investments in 2026, including a $30 billion stake in OpenAI, $3.2 billion in Corning, and $2.1 billion in IREN, cementing its dual role as both chip supplier and AI ecosystem investor.

## Detailed Updates

### Anthropic's Wall Street Push with Finance Agents and Opus 4.7
Anthropic made a major play for the financial services sector, releasing ten ready-to-run agent templates for common banking workflows including pitch building, KYC screening, earnings review, and month-end closing. The company also announced full Microsoft 365 integration, letting Claude carry context across Excel, PowerPoint, Word, and Outlook simultaneously. A marquee data partnership with Moody's embeds credit ratings and risk data for over 600 million companies directly into Claude. The initiative was backed by endorsements from JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon and a $1.5 billion raise alongside Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs.

### Pentagon Signs AI Deals, Cuts Anthropic Out
The Pentagon finalized AI agreements with OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and four other companies to integrate AI capabilities across defense operations. Anthropic was excluded after reportedly refusing to allow the military to use Claude for "all lawful purposes," including autonomous weapons and mass surveillance applications. The move highlights the growing tension between AI safety commitments and government contract requirements.

### NVIDIA's $40B+ AI Investment Spree
NVIDIA is aggressively expanding beyond hardware into AI ecosystem investing, with commitments exceeding $40 billion in 2026. The headline investment is a $30 billion stake in OpenAI, alongside significant positions in Corning ($3.2B) and IREN ($2.1B). This investment strategy positions NVIDIA as a kingmaker in the AI industry, with financial ties across the entire value chain from chips to models to infrastructure.

### AMI Labs Raises $1B Seed Round Led by Yann LeCun
Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, founded by Turing Award winner Yann LeCun, raised $1.03 billion in seed funding at a $3.5 billion valuation. Backed by NVIDIA and Bezos Expeditions, AMI Labs aims to push beyond current LLM architectures toward more fundamental advances in machine intelligence. It is one of the largest seed rounds in AI history.

### Google Remy: AI Personal Agent in Testing
Google is internally testing a new AI personal agent called Remy inside an unreleased version of Gemini. Remy is designed to perform actions on users' behalf across work and personal tasks while learning user preferences over time. The agent represents Google's move toward more autonomous, action-taking AI assistants ahead of the expected Gemini 4 announcement at Google I/O on May 19.

### U.S. Government Expands AI Model Oversight
The Center for AI Standards and Innovation announced pre-release evaluation agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI, while OpenAI and Anthropic renegotiated existing partnerships to align with the Trump administration's AI Action Plan priorities. The agreements give the government early access to evaluate frontier models before public release.

### OpenAI Grants EU Access to Cyber Model
OpenAI announced it would provide the European Union with access to GPT-5.5-Cyber, a specialized variant of its latest model optimized for cybersecurity applications. Meanwhile, Anthropic's Mythos model — which excels at identifying software security flaws — remains restricted to select companies through the company's Project Glasswing cybersecurity initiative.

### Open-Source Model Race Heats Up
The frontier open-source landscape continues to intensify. Zhipu AI's GLM-5.1 targets agentic engineering tasks, while Xiaomi's MiMo-V2.5 focuses on agentic coding and multimodal workflows. DeepSeek V4 is attracting attention with output pricing roughly 34x cheaper than GPT-5.5, and models like Kimi K2.5 and Qwen3 VL now compete at frontier levels.
