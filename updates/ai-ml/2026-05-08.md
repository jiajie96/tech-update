# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 8, 2026

## Highlights

- **Anthropic Commits $200B to Google Cloud**: Anthropic has agreed to pay Google $200 billion over five years for cloud computing and chip access, marking one of the largest infrastructure deals in AI history and underscoring the escalating cost of frontier model development.
- **Pentagon AI Contracts Reshape Industry**: The U.S. Department of Defense finalized AI agreements with eight major tech companies including OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, and Nvidia, while Anthropic was notably excluded — though a federal judge subsequently blocked the government's blacklisting effort.
- **LightSeek Foundation Launches TokenSpeed**: LightSeek Foundation released TokenSpeed, an open-source LLM inference engine targeting TensorRT-LLM-level performance specifically optimized for agentic workloads.

## Detailed Updates

### Anthropic's Massive Google Cloud Deal
Anthropic committed to paying Google $200 billion over five years for cloud and chip access, a deal that highlights how compute infrastructure has become the critical bottleneck in AI development. This partnership ensures Anthropic has the GPU capacity needed to train and serve its next generation of models while deepening ties with Google Cloud's ecosystem.

### Pentagon Finalizes AI Agreements with Big Tech
The Department of Defense signed AI deals with OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, and four other companies, signaling a major expansion of military AI adoption. Anthropic was excluded from the agreements, but a federal judge in California blocked the government's effort to blacklist the company after Anthropic filed suit. The situation underscores the growing tension between AI safety postures and defense contracting.

### Claude Mythos Preview and Cybersecurity
Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview model drew attention for its advanced capability in identifying software vulnerabilities. The model was the first to clear a 32-step end-to-end cyber-attack range, with OpenAI's GPT-5.5 following three weeks later. Anthropic limited the rollout under a new cybersecurity initiative called Project Glasswing, reflecting growing caution around dual-use capabilities in frontier models.

### U.S. Government Moves on AI Oversight
The Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) announced agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI to evaluate their AI models before public release. Meanwhile, the White House is weighing an executive order to formalize federal vetting of all new AI models before market deployment, potentially creating a working group of tech executives and government officials to design the oversight process.

### OpenAI's $122B Fundraise
OpenAI closed a $122 billion funding round at an $852 billion post-money valuation, with Amazon, Nvidia, SoftBank, and Microsoft as anchor investors. This makes OpenAI one of the most valuable private companies in history and provides significant runway for compute infrastructure and model development.

### LightSeek Foundation Releases TokenSpeed
LightSeek Foundation released TokenSpeed, an open-source LLM inference engine designed to match TensorRT-LLM performance levels specifically for agentic workloads. The engine targets the growing demand for efficient inference as AI agents become more prevalent in production environments.

### New Open-Source Models from China
Chinese AI labs continue to push the frontier of open-source models. Zhipu AI's GLM-5.1 targets agentic engineering and complex software development, while Xiaomi's MiMo-V2.5 family offers specialized models for agentic coding and multimodal workflows, with the flagship MiMo-V2.5-Pro featuring 1.02 trillion total parameters with 42 billion active.

### Novo Nordisk Partners with OpenAI
Danish pharmaceutical giant Novo Nordisk announced a strategic partnership with OpenAI to integrate AI across its entire business — from drug discovery and clinical trials to manufacturing and commercial operations. Full deployment is planned by end of 2026, with the goal of accelerating identification of new obesity and diabetes treatments.

### University of Pennsylvania's Mollifier Layers
Researchers at UPenn introduced "Mollifier Layers," a technique that integrates classical mathematical smoothing functions into neural networks for solving inverse partial differential equations. The method has applications spanning genomics, materials science, climate modeling, and chromatin biology, with findings set for publication in Transactions on Machine Learning Research and presentation at NeurIPS 2026.
