# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 4, 2026

## Highlights

- **Pentagon Awards AI Contracts to Seven Companies**: The U.S. Department of Defense awarded classified-network AI contracts to OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, AWS, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI — notably excluding Anthropic over policy disagreements on military use guardrails.
- **Mistral Medium 3.5 Launches**: Mistral AI released its flagship 128B dense model scoring 77.6% on SWE-Bench Verified, alongside new remote coding agents in their Vibe platform and an agentic Work mode in Le Chat.
- **AMI Labs Raises $1.03B Seed Round**: Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, founded by Turing Award winner Yann LeCun, secured a record $1.03 billion in seed funding at a $3.5 billion valuation.

## Detailed Updates

### Pentagon AI Contracts Reshape Industry Dynamics

The Pentagon cleared seven tech companies to deploy AI on its classified networks on May 1, 2026 — OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, AWS, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI (with Oracle added hours later). Anthropic was excluded after refusing to allow Pentagon use of its models for "all lawful" purposes, citing risks around domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. Google's involvement sparked internal backlash, with 950 employees signing an open letter opposing the deal. The contracts mark a significant shift in how frontier AI labs relate to defense applications.

### Mistral AI Ships Medium 3.5 with Remote Agents

Mistral AI launched Mistral Medium 3.5, a dense 128B model with a 256k context window that handles instruction-following, reasoning, and coding in a single set of weights. It achieves 77.6% on SWE-Bench Verified, outperforming Devstral 2 and Qwen3.5 397B. Alongside the model, Mistral announced remote coding agents in Vibe — async cloud sessions that can be spawned from CLI or Le Chat, with the ability to teleport local sessions to the cloud. The new Work mode in Le Chat runs multi-step tasks calling tools in parallel.

### AMI Labs Secures Record Seed Funding

Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, led by Yann LeCun, closed a $1.03 billion seed round at a $3.5 billion valuation. The lab is focused on developing next-generation AI architectures that move beyond current autoregressive language model paradigms, building on LeCun's long-standing research into world models and self-supervised learning.

### Anthropic Approaches $900B+ Valuation

Reports indicate Anthropic is in discussions for a funding round that could value the company at over $900 billion, with the round potentially closing within weeks. This follows Google's announced plan to invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic, reflecting the enormous capital flowing into frontier AI development.

### Sakana AI Introduces KAME Speech Architecture

Tokyo-based Sakana AI released KAME (Knowledge-Access Model Extension), a tandem speech-to-speech architecture that maintains near-zero response latency while injecting LLM knowledge in real time. The system runs a front-end speech model and a back-end LLM asynchronously in parallel — responding immediately while continuously refining outputs. The paper was accepted at ICASSP 2026.

### OpenAI Smartphone Reportedly in Development

Reports suggest OpenAI is developing a smartphone with MediaTek and Qualcomm building the custom chip. The device would use AI agents to complete tasks and maintain continuous context through a combination of on-device and cloud models, representing a potential move into hardware by the AI lab.

### Novo Nordisk Partners with OpenAI

Novo Nordisk announced a strategic partnership with OpenAI to integrate AI across its entire business operations — from drug discovery and clinical trials to manufacturing, supply chains, and commercial operations — with full deployment planned by end of 2026.

### Industry Enters Tougher Phase

Market analysts note that AI is entering a more difficult phase as the initial excitement fades, bringing harder questions about compute costs, hardware access, safety frameworks, and demonstrating concrete business value beyond generic chat applications.
