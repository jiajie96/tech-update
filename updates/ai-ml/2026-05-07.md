# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 7, 2026

## Highlights

- **Anthropic's ARR Surpasses OpenAI**: Anthropic's annual recurring revenue has reached $30 billion, eclipsing OpenAI's $24 billion for the first time, marking a significant shift in the competitive landscape of frontier AI labs.
- **Pentagon AI Deals Reshape Industry Dynamics**: The Pentagon finalized AI agreements with eight major tech companies including OpenAI, Google, and Microsoft, while notably excluding Anthropic over disagreements about military use terms.
- **Anthropic Commits $200B to Google Cloud**: Anthropic signed a landmark five-year, $200 billion commitment to Google for cloud computing and chip access, contributing to a $2 trillion revenue backlog among major cloud providers.

## Detailed Updates

### Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI in Revenue
Anthropic's annual recurring revenue has eclipsed OpenAI's for the first time, with Anthropic reaching $30 billion ARR compared to OpenAI's $24 billion. The milestone reflects the rapid enterprise adoption of Anthropic's models, particularly in financial services and cybersecurity applications. Meanwhile, Anthropic is closing a $1.5 billion funding round from Blackstone, Goldman Sachs, and Hellman & Friedman.

### Mythos Model and Cybersecurity Implications
Anthropic's Mythos model has emerged as a focal point in the AI policy debate after demonstrating unprecedented capabilities in identifying software vulnerabilities. Through Project Glasswing, a controlled initiative giving select organizations access to the model, it identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across major operating systems and browsers within weeks. The model has been classified as a "restricted" frontier model due to its autonomous hacking capabilities, with the NSA and Office of the National Cyber Director expected to lead safety testing.

### Pentagon Signs AI Deals, Excludes Anthropic
The U.S. Department of Defense finalized AI agreements with OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and four other companies. Anthropic was excluded after refusing terms that would allow military use of its models for "all lawful purposes," including autonomous weapons. However, the White House has since reopened discussions with Anthropic following the Mythos announcement, with CEO Dario Amodei meeting senior administration officials.

### Google DeepMind Workers Unionize
Google DeepMind workers in the UK voted to unionize following the company's Pentagon AI contract, in what unions describe as a frontier-lab first. The unionization effort was driven by concerns about military applications of AI research after Google dropped earlier pledges regarding weapons development.

### U.S. Government to Stress-Test AI Models
The Center for AI Standards and Innovation announced agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and Elon Musk's xAI to evaluate their AI models before public release. The initiative represents a growing push toward formal government oversight of frontier AI capabilities.

### Novo Nordisk Partners with OpenAI
Novo Nordisk announced a strategic partnership with OpenAI to integrate AI across its operations, from drug discovery to manufacturing. The deal aims to accelerate identification of new obesity and diabetes treatments by applying AI to the pharmaceutical company's extensive research datasets.

### AI-Generated Content Ruled Ineligible for Oscars
The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences announced that AI-generated actors and scripts are now ineligible for Academy Awards, marking a significant institutional stance on the boundaries between human and machine creativity in the entertainment industry.

### Stanford AI Index 2026 Report
Stanford's Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence released its 2026 AI Index report, highlighting twelve key takeaways about the current state of the field, including the rapid pace of model improvement, growing concerns about safety, and the expanding gap between frontier labs and the broader research community.
