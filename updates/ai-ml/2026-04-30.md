# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — April 30, 2026

## Highlights

- **OpenAI ships GPT-5.5**: Released April 23, the new flagship model scores 88.7% on SWE-bench Verified and 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0, with a 12-million-token context window and 60% fewer hallucinations than GPT-5.
- **DeepSeek V4 drops as open-weight frontier model**: Two variants — V4-Pro (1.6T params) and V4-Flash (284B params) — released under MIT license at dramatically low prices, redefining the cost curve for frontier AI.
- **Google commits up to $40 billion to Anthropic**: The largest single AI investment ever recorded, deepening the strategic partnership between the two companies.

## Detailed Updates

### OpenAI GPT-5.5 Launch
OpenAI released GPT-5.5 (codename "Spud") on April 23, available to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise ChatGPT users. The model posts 88.7% on SWE-bench Verified, 92.4% on MMLU, and 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0. API pricing sits at $5/M input tokens and $30/M output tokens for the base model, with a Pro variant at $30/$180. GPT-5.5 features a 1M-token API context window and focuses on agentic task completion — writing code, researching online, and operating software end-to-end.

### DeepSeek V4 Preview Goes Open-Source
DeepSeek released V4 on April 24 under the MIT license. V4-Pro runs 1.6 trillion total parameters with 49B active (MoE), while V4-Flash uses 284B total with 13B active. Both support 1M context and dual thinking/non-thinking modes. V4-Flash is priced at $0.14/M input tokens — making it the cheapest frontier-class model ever publicly released. V4-Pro beats all open models in math, STEM, and coding benchmarks, trailing only Gemini 3.1-Pro for world knowledge.

### Anthropic Claude Mythos Preview
Anthropic previewed Claude Mythos to roughly 50 partner organizations through Project Glasswing, focusing on cybersecurity vulnerability detection, reasoning, and coding. Described as a "step change" above Claude Opus 4.6, Mythos has not yet been made publicly available.

### AMI Labs Raises $1.03 Billion Seed Round
Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, founded by Turing Award winner Yann LeCun, raised $1.03 billion in seed funding at a $3.5 billion valuation — the largest seed round in European history. The Paris-based startup is building "world models," an alternative AI architecture that learns by understanding how the physical world works rather than relying on language prediction.

### Google-Thinking Machines Lab Deal
Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab signed a multibillion-dollar deal with Google Cloud for AI infrastructure powered by Nvidia's GB300 chips. The partnership gives the startup access to Google's latest AI systems and deepens the competitive landscape among frontier labs.

### Allen Institute BAR Post-Training Recipe
The Allen Institute for AI introduced "BAR," a post-training recipe enabling domain-specific experts to be trained separately and merged into a single Mixture-of-Experts model. This modular approach lets developers upgrade specific capabilities without retraining entire foundation systems, significantly reducing costs.

### Physics-Informed ML Breakthrough
Researchers at the University of Hawaii unveiled a new algorithm for physics-informed machine learning that allows AI to adhere to physical laws while processing complex datasets. The method improves prediction accuracy in fluid dynamics and climate modeling applications.

### Google Gemma 4 Family
Google released the Gemma 4 family under Apache 2.0, delivering four models for different deployment scenarios. The flagship Gemma 4 31B Dense reportedly outperforms models 20 times its size on key benchmarks, offering strong open-source options for developers.
