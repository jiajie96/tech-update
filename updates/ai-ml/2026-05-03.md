# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 3, 2026

## Highlights

- **Google invests up to $40B in Anthropic**: Google announced a massive investment deal — $10B upfront with up to $30B more tied to performance milestones — while Anthropic explores a separate raise at a $900B valuation, surpassing OpenAI's $852B figure from March.
- **AMI Labs raises record $1.03B seed round**: Yann LeCun's Paris-based startup, building "world models" as an alternative to LLMs, secured the largest seed round in European history at a $3.5B valuation.
- **Novo Nordisk partners with OpenAI**: The pharma giant will integrate AI across drug discovery, clinical trials, manufacturing, and commercial operations, with full deployment planned by year-end.

## Detailed Updates

### Google's $40B Anthropic Investment Reshapes AI Landscape
Google plans to invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic, with $10 billion committed immediately and the remaining amount contingent on performance milestones. The deal is especially significant given that nearly half of Alphabet's record $62.6 billion quarterly profit — roughly $28.7 billion — came from revaluing its private equity holdings, primarily in Anthropic. Amazon reported similar gains of $16.8 billion from its Anthropic stake. Meanwhile, Anthropic is separately in talks with investors at a $900 billion valuation.

### AMI Labs' Record-Breaking $1.03B Seed Round
Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, founded by Turing Award winner Yann LeCun, raised $1.03 billion in seed funding at a $3.5 billion valuation — the largest seed round in European history. The Paris-based startup is developing "world models," an alternative AI architecture that learns by understanding how the physical world works, with applications targeting robotics, healthcare, and manufacturing.

### OpenAI and Microsoft Restructure Partnership
OpenAI and Microsoft have quietly restructured their exclusive arrangement. OpenAI can now sell its services across AWS and Google Cloud, ending the exclusivity that had been a hallmark of the partnership. This move diversifies OpenAI's distribution channels and signals a maturing business strategy.

### New Model Releases: Claude Mythos 5 and Gemini 3.1
Anthropic unveiled Claude Mythos 5, a 10-trillion-parameter model excelling at cybersecurity, coding, and academic reasoning, alongside a mid-sized model called Capabara. Google DeepMind released Gemini 3.1, a real-time multimodal AI capable of processing voice and visual data simultaneously, targeting healthcare and customer service applications.

### Mistral AI Launches 128B Flagship and Agentic Mode
Mistral AI released its latest update featuring async cloud-based coding sessions, a new 128B-parameter flagship model, and an agentic "Work" mode for Le Chat. The release continues Mistral's strategy of positioning itself as a competitive European alternative to US-based AI labs.

### MIT's FTTE Framework Accelerates Federated Learning
MIT researchers introduced FTTE, a framework that speeds federated learning by 81% while cutting memory overhead by 80% and communication payload by 69%. The breakthrough addresses key bottlenecks in privacy-preserving distributed training.

### OpenAI Exploring Smartphone Hardware
Reports indicate OpenAI may be developing a smartphone in partnership with MediaTek and Qualcomm, with the device designed around AI agents that maintain continuous context through on-device and cloud models. The move would represent a significant hardware expansion for the AI lab.

### GPT-5.2 and Inference-Time Scaling
GPT-5.2 has arrived with a significantly larger context window and improved performance on code and multi-step workflows. The broader industry trend in 2026 is toward inference-time scaling — spending more compute during generation rather than training — to boost output quality.
