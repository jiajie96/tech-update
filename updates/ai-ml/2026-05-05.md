# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 5, 2026

## Highlights

- **Pentagon finalizes AI deals with 8 tech giants, excludes Anthropic**: The U.S. Department of Defense signed AI agreements with OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Oracle, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI, while labeling Anthropic a supply-chain risk — though White House discussions have since reopened.
- **AMI Labs raises $1.03B for "world models"**: Founded by Turing Award winner Yann LeCun, Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs secured massive seed funding at a $3.5B valuation to develop an alternative AI architecture that learns by understanding how the physical world works.
- **Google invests up to $40B in Anthropic**: In a landmark deal combining cash and compute, Google Cloud will provide Anthropic with 5 gigawatts of capacity over the next five years.

## Detailed Updates

### Pentagon AI Contracts Reshape Industry Landscape

The Pentagon finalized artificial intelligence agreements with eight major companies — OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Oracle, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI. Notably, Anthropic was excluded after a contract dispute led the Defense Department to label it a supply-chain risk. However, the White House has since reopened discussions following Anthropic's recent technology breakthroughs and the release of its Mythos model.

### AMI Labs Raises Record $1.03 Billion Seed Round

Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, founded by Yann LeCun, raised $1.03 billion in seed funding at a $3.5 billion valuation. The company is developing "world models" — an alternative to large language models that learns by understanding physical-world dynamics rather than text prediction. This represents one of the largest seed rounds in AI history.

### Google's Massive Anthropic Investment

Google announced plans to invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic through a combination of cash and compute resources. Google Cloud will provide 5 gigawatts of computing capacity over five years, with room to scale further. This cements Google's position as Anthropic's largest infrastructure partner.

### OpenAI Secures $10B Wall Street Funding

OpenAI secured $10 billion in funding from a 19-firm Wall Street consortium, while Anthropic is separately closing $1.5 billion from Blackstone, Goldman Sachs, and Hellman & Friedman. The funding race continues to accelerate as frontier AI companies require ever-larger capital reserves for compute.

### Novo Nordisk Partners with OpenAI

Danish pharmaceutical giant Novo Nordisk announced a strategic partnership with OpenAI to integrate AI across its entire business — from drug discovery and clinical trials to manufacturing — with full deployment planned by end of 2026. This is one of the most comprehensive enterprise AI integrations in the pharmaceutical sector.

### New Open-Source Frontier Models

Several major open-source releases landed this period. Zhipu AI released GLM-5.1, designed for agentic engineering with up to 128K output tokens. Xiaomi launched MiMo-V2.5-Pro (42B active parameters) focused on coding agents and long-horizon tool use. Google released Gemma 4 under Apache 2.0, emphasizing coding, agentic AI, and improved reasoning.

### Mollifier Layers Advance Scientific AI

Researchers at the University of Pennsylvania introduced "Mollifier Layers," integrating classical mathematical smoothing functions into neural networks to solve inverse partial differential equations with greater stability and efficiency — addressing a longstanding challenge in physics-informed machine learning.

### U.S. Air Force Deploys WarMatrix

The U.S. Air Force announced the successful inaugural operational use of WarMatrix, an AI-powered wargaming environment capable of running simulations up to 10,000 times faster than real time, marking a significant milestone in military AI applications.
