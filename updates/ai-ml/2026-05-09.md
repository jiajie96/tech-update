# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 9, 2026

## Highlights

- **Anthropic's Mythos Preview and $50B Fundraise**: Anthropic unveiled Claude Mythos Preview, a model specializing in cybersecurity vulnerability detection, while simultaneously pursuing a funding round of up to $50 billion that would value the company at roughly $900 billion. CEO Dario Amodei met with senior Trump administration officials to discuss Mythos deployment under Project Glasswing.
- **OpenAI GPT-5.4 Ships with 1M-Token Context**: OpenAI released GPT-5.4 featuring a 1-million-token context window and autonomous multi-step workflow execution, scoring 75% on the OSWorld-V desktop productivity benchmark. The company has surpassed $25 billion in annualized revenue and is eyeing a potential public listing by late 2026.
- **Apple Adopts Google Gemini for Foundation Models**: Apple and Google announced a multi-year collaboration under which Apple's next-generation Foundation Models will be built on Google's Gemini architecture and cloud infrastructure, while iOS 27 will allow users to choose from multiple third-party AI models for the first time.

## Detailed Updates

### Anthropic Mythos Preview and White House Engagement
Anthropic launched Claude Mythos Preview, a powerful model optimized for identifying software security vulnerabilities. The company limited the initial rollout to select enterprise partners as part of Project Glasswing, a new cybersecurity initiative. Separately, Anthropic is pursuing a massive funding round of up to $50 billion, which would value the company near $900 billion. The company is approaching $19 billion in annualized revenue.

### OpenAI GPT-5.4 with Autonomous Workflows
OpenAI unveiled GPT-5.4 with a 1-million-token context window and the ability to autonomously execute multi-step workflows across software environments. The model scored 75% on the OSWorld-V benchmark, which simulates real desktop productivity tasks. OpenAI has now surpassed $25 billion in annualized revenue and is reportedly exploring an IPO timeline for late 2026.

### Apple-Google AI Partnership and iOS 27
In a landmark shift, Apple announced that iOS 27 will let users select from multiple third-party AI models for text, editing, and image tasks — breaking the iPhone's two-year OpenAI exclusivity. Additionally, Apple's next-generation Foundation Models will be built on Google's Gemini models and cloud infrastructure through a multi-year collaboration.

### Google Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite
Google introduced Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, an efficiency-focused model delivering 2.5× faster response times and 45% faster output generation compared to earlier Gemini versions, targeting cost-sensitive enterprise deployments.

### Pentagon AI Deals Exclude Anthropic
The Pentagon finalized AI agreements with eight major tech companies including OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, and Nvidia, notably excluding Anthropic. Hundreds of employees at Google and OpenAI subsequently signed letters supporting Anthropic's position in the Pentagon dispute.

### Trump Administration AI Model Testing
The Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) announced new agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI to evaluate their AI models before public release, building on earlier partnerships with OpenAI and Anthropic.

### AMI Labs Raises $1B for World Models
Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, founded by Yann LeCun, raised $1.03 billion in seed funding at a $3.5 billion valuation. The company is building "world models," an alternative architecture to LLMs that learns by understanding physical-world dynamics.

### OpenAI Infrastructure Spending at $50B
OpenAI President Greg Brockman testified before the U.S. Senate that OpenAI expects to spend approximately $50 billion on infrastructure in 2026, a dramatic increase from $30 million in 2017.

### Cloudflare LLM Infrastructure
Cloudflare announced purpose-built high-performance infrastructure for running LLMs at the edge, providing enterprises with lower-latency inference options outside of hyperscaler environments.
