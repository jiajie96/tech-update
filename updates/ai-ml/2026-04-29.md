# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — April 29, 2026

## Highlights

- **OpenAI Ships GPT-5.5**: Released on April 23, GPT-5.5 scores 88.7% on SWE-bench Verified and ships with a 12-million-token context window, along with a "Pro" variant for higher-accuracy workloads. Pricing starts at $5/1M input tokens.
- **DeepSeek V4 Drops in Open-Weight Preview**: The Chinese lab released V4-Pro (1.6T parameters, 49B activated) and V4-Flash (284B parameters, 13B activated), both open-source with 1M token context — requiring only 27% of V3's inference FLOPs.
- **Google Commits Up to $40 Billion to Anthropic**: The largest single AI investment on record, with $10B upfront at a $350B valuation and another $30B tied to performance milestones, alongside up to five gigawatts of compute capacity over five years.

## Detailed Updates

### OpenAI GPT-5.5 Launch
OpenAI released GPT-5.5 on April 23 with headline improvements in agentic coding, computer use, and knowledge work. The model features a 1M-token context window (with 12M-token extended context), scores 92.4% on MMLU, and claims a 60% reduction in hallucinations versus GPT-5.4. A higher-accuracy GPT-5.5 Pro variant is available at $30/1M input tokens. The release came just weeks after GPT-5.4, signaling an accelerating cadence from OpenAI.

### DeepSeek V4 Open-Weight Preview
On April 24, DeepSeek released V4 in two Mixture-of-Experts variants. V4-Pro packs 1.6 trillion parameters (49B activated per token) while V4-Flash offers a leaner 284B parameters (13B activated). Both support 1M-token context with dramatically improved efficiency — V4-Pro uses only 10% of the KV cache compared to V3.2. The models are fully open-source on Hugging Face, continuing DeepSeek's strategy of democratizing frontier-class models.

### Google's $40B Anthropic Investment
Google announced plans to invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — $10 billion in immediate cash at a $350 billion valuation, with $30 billion more contingent on performance targets. The deal includes access to up to 5 GW of Google Cloud computing capacity over five years. The move came the same week Amazon committed an additional $5 billion to Anthropic, underscoring the intensifying infrastructure arms race.

### Alibaba Qwen 3.6 Max Preview
Alibaba released Qwen 3.6 Max in preview as its most powerful model yet, with significant gains in coding, reasoning, and agentic performance. The release adds another strong contender to an already crowded frontier landscape.

### Meta MTIA Custom Silicon Deployment
Meta announced deployment of its MTIA (Meta Training and Inference Accelerator) chips across its data centers, with the MTIA 400 currently in testing and MTIA 450 and 500 planned for mass deployment by 2027. The move aims to reduce Meta's dependence on NVIDIA GPUs for AI workloads.

### AI Hallucination Research at ICLR 2026
A paper presented at ICLR 2026 revealed a counterintuitive finding: training models to reason harder actually causes them to hallucinate more tool calls. With 96% of enterprises now running AI agents, the discovery raises important questions about the reliability of agentic systems at scale.

### Geoffrey Hinton Calls for AI Governance
Nobel laureate Geoffrey Hinton spoke at the Digital World Conference, comparing current AI development to "a very fast car with no steering wheel" and urging more careful governance of rapid AI advances.
