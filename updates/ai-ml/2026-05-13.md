# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 13, 2026

## Highlights

- **Elon Musk Shuts Down xAI, Hands Compute to Anthropic**: In a stunning move on May 6, Musk announced xAI would no longer operate independently and transferred a large share of his AI compute infrastructure to Anthropic, reshaping the competitive landscape.
- **Pentagon Signs AI Deals with 8 Tech Giants, Excludes Anthropic**: The Defense Department finalized agreements with OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Oracle, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI for classified AI deployments, notably leaving out Anthropic over supply-chain concerns.
- **OpenAI Grants EU Access to GPT-5.5-Cyber**: OpenAI is providing European governments and institutions access to its cybersecurity-focused model variant, while Anthropic continues to withhold its Mythos model from the bloc.

## Detailed Updates

### xAI Dissolution and Anthropic Compute Transfer
On May 6, Elon Musk announced on X that xAI would cease operating as an independent company. A significant portion of xAI's compute resources was transferred to Anthropic, marking a dramatic shift in the AI industry's competitive dynamics. The move effectively consolidates compute capacity among fewer frontier AI players.

### Pentagon Expands AI Use Across Defense
The Pentagon finalized AI partnerships with eight major companies — OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Oracle, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI — to expand AI usage into classified environments supporting analysis, logistics, and large-scale data processing. Anthropic was excluded from the agreements, with the Defense Department citing supply-chain risk concerns following a contract dispute.

### OpenAI Cybersecurity Model for Europe
OpenAI announced it would grant EU partners access to GPT-5.5-Cyber, a cybersecurity-focused variant of its latest model. European businesses, governments, and institutions including the EU AI Office will receive access. Meanwhile, Anthropic has delayed its Mythos model rollout to Europe over concerns about vulnerability exploitation.

### US Government AI Oversight Expands
The Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) announced new agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI to conduct pre-deployment evaluations of frontier AI models. This builds on earlier partnerships with OpenAI and Anthropic from 2024, signaling an expanding government role in AI safety testing.

### Google Tests "Remy" Personal AI Agent
Google is internally testing a new AI personal agent called Remy within Gemini, designed to autonomously perform tasks across Gmail, Calendar, Docs, Drive, and Android services. The agent represents Google's push to evolve Gemini from a conversational AI into an autonomous task-execution platform.

### Apple-Google Foundation Model Partnership
Apple and Google announced a multi-year collaboration under which Apple's next generation of Apple Foundation Models will be built on Google's Gemini models and cloud technology, representing a significant strategic alliance in the foundation model space.

### Qwen 3.6 Dominates Coding Benchmarks
Alibaba's Qwen 3.6 Max-Preview holds the number one position on six major coding and agent benchmarks simultaneously, including SWE-bench Pro and Terminal-Bench 2.0. Meanwhile, Kimi K2.6, a 1.1T mixture-of-experts model, leads as the top open-weight model on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index.

### DeepSeek V4 Pricing Disrupts Market
DeepSeek V4 is priced at roughly 34x cheaper output than GPT-5.5, with V4-Flash offering output at $0.07 per million tokens post-promo — the cheapest frontier-class API price available. The aggressive pricing is making the Western vs. Chinese cost gap the defining economics story of the AI industry this month.

### AMI Labs Raises $1B for World Models
Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, founded by Turing Award winner Yann LeCun, raised $1.03 billion in seed funding at a $3.5 billion valuation. The company is developing "world models" as an alternative AI architecture to large language models.

### OpenAI Launches Consulting Firm
OpenAI launched OpenAI Deployment Co., a consulting firm to help organizations build and deploy AI systems, following Anthropic's earlier formation of a similar venture with Wall Street financial services firms.
