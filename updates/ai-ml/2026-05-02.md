# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — May 2, 2026

## Highlights

- **Pentagon Signs AI Deals with Seven Tech Giants**: The Department of Defense inked AI procurement agreements with AWS, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, OpenAI, SpaceX, and Reflection AI for classified network deployment — notably excluding Anthropic, which was designated a supply chain risk in February.
- **DeepSeek V4 Challenges GPT-5.5**: DeepSeek released V4 Flash and V4 Pro with aggressive pricing and open-weight access, intensifying competition in the foundation model space just days after OpenAI's GPT-5.5 launch.
- **MIT's FTTE Framework Revolutionizes Federated Learning**: A new framework from MIT speeds federated learning by 81% while cutting memory overhead by 80%, enabling privacy-preserving AI training on resource-constrained edge devices.

## Detailed Updates

### Pentagon Clears Seven Companies for Classified AI Deployment
The Pentagon announced agreements with Amazon Web Services, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, OpenAI, SpaceX, and Reflection AI to deploy AI products on its Impact Level 6 and 7 classified networks. Oracle was added hours later as an eighth partner. The tools will be available via GenAI.mil, the DoD's central AI platform. Anthropic was notably absent after being designated a supply chain risk by the Trump administration in February. Reflection AI, founded by former Google DeepMind researchers, was the only non-household name on the list.

### DeepSeek V4 Launches with Aggressive Pricing
DeepSeek responded to OpenAI's GPT-5.5 (released April 23) with its V4 Flash and V4 Pro models, offering open-weight access and competitive pricing that undercuts Western competitors. The release continues the trend of Chinese AI labs closing the gap with frontier Western models while maintaining significantly lower costs. V4 Pro targets enterprise and research use cases with strong agentic capabilities.

### Anthropic Ships Claude Opus 4.7
Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.7 in mid-April, achieving a jump on SWE-bench Pro from 53.4% to 64.3% and CursorBench from 58% to 70%. Vision resolution tripled to 3.75 megapixels. The company is expected to follow with Claude Sonnet 4.8 in May, continuing its rapid release cadence.

### Google Gemma 4 Goes Open Source
Google released Gemma 4 on April 2 under the Apache 2.0 license in four sizes: E2B, E4B, 26B MoE, and 31B Dense. The models are purpose-built for advanced reasoning and agentic workflows, natively processing video and images at variable resolutions. The 31B model currently ranks #3 among open models on the Arena AI text leaderboard, and all models support 140+ languages with up to 256K context windows.

### MIT FTTE Framework Transforms Edge AI Training
MIT researchers introduced the Federated Tiny Training Engine (FTTE), a framework that enables privacy-preserving AI training on everyday devices like smartwatches and sensors. FTTE achieves 81% faster training, 80% less memory overhead, and 69% reduced communication payload compared to standard federated learning. The framework uses sparse parameter updates and staleness-weighted aggregation to handle heterogeneous devices with intermittent connectivity.

### AMI Labs' $1B Bet on World Models
Yann LeCun's AMI Labs, founded after his departure from Meta, raised $1.03 billion at a $3.5 billion valuation — the largest seed round for a European startup. The Paris-based company is building world models using JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture), which learns abstract representations of reality rather than predicting tokens. Investors include Bezos Expeditions, Eric Schmidt, and Mark Cuban.

### Google TurboQuant Reduces LLM Memory at ICLR 2026
Google's research team presented TurboQuant at ICLR 2026, an algorithm that significantly reduces memory overhead from the KV cache in large language models. The method combines PolarQuant vector rotation with the Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss compression method, enabling more efficient inference without meaningful accuracy loss.
