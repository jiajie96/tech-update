# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — April 28, 2026

## Highlights

- **Model release frenzy**: GPT-5.5, DeepSeek V4 Preview, Claude Opus 4.7, and Gemma 4 all shipped within weeks of each other, marking the most competitive stretch in foundation model history.
- **Google commits up to $40B in Anthropic**: A landmark investment based on a $380B valuation signals the scale of capital now flowing into frontier AI labs.
- **AI agents hit inflection point**: Stanford's AI Index reports agent success on real-world computer tasks jumped from 12% to 66% year-over-year, validating the industry's pivot toward agentic systems.

## Detailed Updates

### OpenAI Ships GPT-5.5
OpenAI released GPT-5.5 on April 23, the latest in its flagship model series. The model features improvements in multi-step reasoning, tool use, and autonomous workflows. Reviewers note stronger coding performance and more reliable agentic behavior compared to its predecessor, continuing OpenAI's cadence of incremental but meaningful capability jumps.

### DeepSeek V4 Preview Drops at Aggressive Pricing
Just 24 hours after GPT-5.5, DeepSeek released V4 Preview — a trillion-parameter open-source model built entirely on Huawei Ascend chips. The Flash variant is priced at $0.14 per million input tokens, dramatically undercutting proprietary competitors. The model's reliance on Chinese-made silicon rather than Nvidia hardware demonstrates an alternative supply chain for large-scale training, raising questions about the long-term effectiveness of export controls.

### Anthropic Launches Claude Opus 4.7
Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.7 on April 16, designed for complex reasoning and long-running agent workflows. The model targets enterprise use cases where sustained multi-step problem-solving is critical. Separately, Anthropic announced Claude Mythos Preview on April 7, currently available to roughly 50 partner organizations and focused on cybersecurity, advanced reasoning, and coding applications.

### Google Gemma 4 Family Goes Open Source
Google released the Gemma 4 model family on April 2 under the Apache 2.0 license, offering four models tailored to different deployment scenarios. The standout is the 31B dense model, which outperforms models 20 times its size on multiple benchmarks. The open-source release intensifies competition with Chinese labs that have dominated the open-weight space.

### GLM-5.1 and Microsoft Enter the Fray
Zhipu's GLM-5.1 claims to beat the best proprietary models on SWE-Bench Pro, a challenging software engineering benchmark. Meanwhile, Microsoft announced three new foundational models spanning text, voice, and image generation, continuing to build a proprietary multimodal stack alongside its OpenAI partnership.

### Google Plans Up to $40B Anthropic Investment
Google is planning to invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic, with $10 billion upfront and up to $30 billion contingent on milestones, based on a $380 billion valuation. This dwarfs previous AI investments and cements the Google-Anthropic relationship as a central axis of the AI industry. Anthropic also secured 5 gigawatts of computing capacity through deals with Google and Broadcom.

### Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab Signs Google Cloud Deal
Thinking Machines Lab, founded by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati, signed a multibillion-dollar agreement with Google Cloud for AI infrastructure, including access to Nvidia GB300 chips. Combined with AMI Labs raising a $1.03 billion seed round at $3.5 billion valuation, the capital flowing into next-generation AI startups continues to accelerate.

### Frontier Labs Unite Against Model Copying
OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google are collaborating through the Frontier Model Forum to combat unauthorized model copying in China. Internally, competition remains fierce — Sergey Brin has assembled a DeepMind "strike team" specifically to close the gap with Anthropic's coding capabilities, underscoring how coding performance has become a key battleground.

### Research: Physics-Informed ML and Confidence Calibration
A University of Hawaii team advanced physics-informed machine learning algorithms that enable models to respect physical laws while processing complex datasets. Separately, a new training method improves AI confidence estimates without sacrificing task performance, addressing a persistent challenge in deploying reasoning models in high-stakes settings.
