## Detailed Updates

### DeepSeek V4 Preview Release (April 24)

DeepSeek released preview versions of V4-Pro and V4-Flash, its latest flagship models. V4-Pro features 1.6 trillion total parameters with 49 billion active per forward pass, while V4-Flash offers a lighter 284B/13B configuration. Both support one million token context windows through a novel hybrid attention mechanism combining Compressed Sparse Attention and Heavily Compressed Attention. V4-Pro requires only 27% of inference FLOPs and 10% of KV cache compared to DeepSeek-V3.2. On benchmarks, V4-Pro beats all rival open models for math and coding, trailing only Google's closed Gemini 3.1-Pro for world knowledge. Pricing is notably aggressive at $0.14/M input tokens for Flash and $1.74/M for Pro. Both models are open-source under the MIT license.

### OpenAI Releases GPT-5.5 "Spud" (April 23)

OpenAI shipped its latest model on April 23, branding it GPT-5.5 rather than the widely anticipated GPT-6. Codenamed "Spud," the model is described as a "faster, sharper thinker for fewer tokens" compared to GPT-5.4. It excels at multi-step agentic workflows — writing and debugging code, researching online, analyzing data, and operating software autonomously. OpenAI reports a 60% reduction in hallucinations versus GPT-5.4 on internal benchmarks, though third-party testing from Artificial Analysis shows more mixed results. GPT-5.5 is available to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise users, with API access following on April 24. OpenAI also launched a Bio Bug Bounty program offering $25,000 for the first researcher to find a universal jailbreak, running April 28 to July 27.

### ChatGPT Images 2.0 (April 21)

OpenAI launched ChatGPT Images 2.0, the first OpenAI image model with native reasoning capabilities. Key improvements include ~99% text accuracy in any language or script, built-in reasoning before generating, context-aware multi-turn editing, support for 100+ objects in a single scene, and 2K resolution with flexible aspect ratios. The model scored 1,512 on the Image Arena leaderboard — a 242-point lead over the second-place model. DALL-E 2 and DALL-E 3 are being retired on May 12, 2026.

### TurboQuant Presented at ICLR 2026 (April 25)

Google's TurboQuant was formally presented at ICLR 2026 in Rio de Janeiro. The algorithm achieves 6x KV cache compression at 3 bits per channel with near-zero quality loss, using a two-stage approach of PolarQuant rotation followed by 1-bit QJL residual correction. On NVIDIA H100 GPUs, 4-bit TurboQuant accelerates attention logit computation by up to 8x compared to unquantized keys. The technique enables models with massive context windows to run far more efficiently on existing hardware.

Sources: [Google Research Blog](https://research.google/blog/turboquant-redefining-ai-efficiency-with-extreme-compression/) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/google-turboquant-ai-memory-compression-silicon-valley-pied-piper/)

### Brain-Inspired Chip Cuts AI Energy 70% (April 22)

University of Cambridge researchers published a paper in Science Advances describing a nanoelectronic device using modified hafnium oxide that mimics neuronal processing. By adding strontium and titanium to create controlled p-n junctions, the device changes resistance through interface energy barriers rather than unreliable filament formation, potentially slashing AI energy use by up to 70% while enabling more natural learning and adaptation.
