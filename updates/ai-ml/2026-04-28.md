# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — April 28, 2026

## Highlights

- **DeepSeek V4 Launches in Preview**: China's DeepSeek released V4-Pro (1.6T params, 49B active) and V4-Flash (284B params, 13B active), both supporting 1M-token context windows with a novel hybrid attention mechanism that slashes inference costs dramatically.
- **Google Releases Gemma 4 Open Models**: Four model variants under Apache 2.0, with the 31B dense flagship delivering massive benchmark gains — AIME math jumping from 20.8% to 89.2% over Gemma 3.
- **Brain-Inspired Chip Cuts AI Energy Use by 70%**: Researchers engineered a nanoelectronic device using modified hafnium oxide that mimics neuronal processing, potentially transforming edge AI deployment.

## Detailed Updates

### DeepSeek V4 Preview Release

On April 24, DeepSeek released preview versions of its V4-Pro and V4-Flash models, marking a major step forward for open-source AI. V4-Pro features 1.6 trillion total parameters with 49 billion active per forward pass, while V4-Flash offers a lighter 284B/13B configuration. Both models support one million token context windows through a new Compressed Sparse Attention (CSA) and Heavily Compressed Attention (HCA) hybrid mechanism. V4-Pro requires only 27% of inference FLOPs and 10% of KV cache compared to DeepSeek-V3.2. V4-Pro beats all rival open models for math and coding, trailing only Google's closed Gemini 3.1-Pro for world knowledge. Pricing is aggressive: $0.14/M input tokens for Flash and $1.74/M for Pro. Both models are open-source under permissive licensing.

### Google Gemma 4 Family

Released on April 2 under Apache 2.0, Gemma 4 delivers four variants: E2B (2.3B effective), E4B (4.5B effective), 26B MoE (4B active), and 31B dense. The models support context windows up to 256K tokens, native vision and audio processing, and fluency in 140+ languages. Benchmark improvements over Gemma 3 are striking: LiveCodeBench coding jumps from 29.1% to 80.0%, and GPQA science from 42.4% to 84.3%. Google positioned Gemma 4 specifically for edge deployment on phones, consumer GPUs, and devices like Jetson Nano.

### Energy Efficiency Breakthroughs

Two significant energy efficiency advances emerged this month. Researchers developed a nanoelectronic device using modified hafnium oxide that mimics how neurons process and store information, operating at ultra-low power and potentially slashing AI energy use by up to 70%. Separately, a team unveiled a hybrid approach combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning that could reduce AI energy consumption by up to 100x while actually improving accuracy — a potential game-changer for sustainable AI deployment.

### TurboQuant Algorithm at ICLR 2026

Google's research team presented TurboQuant at ICLR 2026, an algorithm that significantly reduces memory overhead from the KV cache using a two-step quantization process. The technique allows models with massive context windows to run far more efficiently, potentially accelerating a broader shift toward efficiency-first AI development across the industry.

### Physics-Informed Machine Learning Advances

Researchers at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa unveiled a new algorithm for physics-informed machine learning that constrains AI models to adhere to physical laws while processing complex datasets. The approach leads to more accurate predictions in fluid dynamics and climate modeling, addressing a longstanding challenge of ensuring AI-generated predictions remain physically plausible.

### OpenAI GPT-6 Still Pending

OpenAI's GPT-6 (codenamed "Spud") missed its rumored April 14 launch window, with Sam Altman indicating it remains a few weeks out. The model reportedly uses a two-tier inference framework — System-1 for rapid responses and System-2 for internal logic verification — with claims of reducing hallucination rates below 0.1%.

### Zhipu AI Releases GLM-5.1

Chinese AI lab Zhipu AI released GLM-5.1 under the MIT license, a 744-billion-parameter mixture-of-experts model with 40 billion active parameters per forward pass and a 200K context window. The model reportedly beats GPT-5.4 on coding benchmarks, underscoring the intensifying competition from open-source Chinese models.
