# AI & Machine Learning Weekly Update — April 27, 2026

## Highlights

- **OpenAI ships GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Pro**: Rolled out April 24 with stronger multi-step reasoning, tool use, coding, and agentic workflows. Testers say it surpasses GPT-5.4 at reasoning and autonomy.
- **Yann LeCun's AMI Labs raises $1.03B seed**: The largest seed-stage investment in European history, building "world models" as an alternative to LLMs at a $3.5B valuation.
- **Snap cuts 1,000 jobs citing AI**: AI now generates 65%+ of Snap's new code; restructuring expected to save $500M+ annually.

## Detailed Updates

### OpenAI GPT-5.5 Launch
On April 24, OpenAI made GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Pro available in the API and ChatGPT. The model features stronger multi-step reasoning, tool use, coding, research, document creation, and agentic workflows. Senior engineers who tested the model said it catches issues in advance and predicts testing/review needs without explicit prompting — a meaningful leap in autonomous capability over GPT-5.4.

### Anthropic Claude Mythos Preview
Announced April 7, Claude Mythos is being tested by roughly 50 partner organizations through Project Glasswing. The model focuses on cybersecurity vulnerability detection, advanced reasoning, and coding. Mozilla used it to patch 271 Firefox vulnerabilities. Separately, Anthropic's ARR exceeded $30 billion in March 2026, and the company expanded its Amazon partnership for up to 5 gigawatts of new compute.

### Yann LeCun Founds AMI Labs with Record Seed Round
Turing Award winner Yann LeCun left Meta in late 2025 and founded Advanced Machine Intelligence (AMI) Labs in Paris. On March 9, the startup announced a $1.03 billion seed round at $3.5B pre-money — the largest seed in European history. AMI Labs is building "world models" that learn from reality rather than language, explicitly rejecting the LLM approach that dominates the industry.

### Google Gemma 4 and Gemini 3.1
Google released the Gemma 4 family on April 2 under Apache 2.0 with four variants (E2B, E4B, 26B MoE, 31B Dense). The flagship 31B Dense hit 89.2% on AIME 2026 and 80.0% on LiveCodeBench v6. Gemini 3.1 Pro leads on abstract reasoning (77.1% on ARC-AGI-2) and science (94.3% on GPQA Diamond) with a 1M-token context window.

### Snap Layoffs Signal AI-Driven Workforce Shift
Snap CEO Evan Spiegel laid off ~1,000 employees and closed 300+ open roles, explicitly citing AI allowing smaller teams to match previous output. AI now generates over 65% of Snap's new code. The restructuring is expected to deliver $500M+ in annualized savings — a stark example of AI's impact on tech employment.

### Chinese Labs Surge with Open-Weight Models
April saw massive releases from Chinese labs: Zhipu GLM-5.1 (744B MoE, MIT license), Alibaba Qwen 3.6-Plus (agentic coding, 1M context), Qwen3.5-Omni scaling to hundreds of billions of parameters with hybrid MoE architecture, and DeepSeek's newest flagship model. Open-weight competition from China is intensifying pressure on proprietary providers.

### Microsoft Launches Three Foundation Models
Microsoft AI announced three new foundational models for text, voice, and image generation, continuing its push to build a proprietary multimodal stack independent of its OpenAI partnership.

### Research: Physics-Informed ML & Confidence Calibration
University of Hawaiʻi researchers advanced physics-informed machine learning, enabling AI to adhere to physical laws while processing complex datasets. A separate breakthrough improves AI confidence estimates without sacrificing performance, addressing hallucination in reasoning models. A new dataset of 30,000+ competition math problems was also released on April 24.

### Record VC Funding: $267.2B in Q1 2026
The first quarter saw record-shattering $267.2 billion in venture funding, dominated by OpenAI, Anthropic, and the xAI acquisition by SpaceX.
