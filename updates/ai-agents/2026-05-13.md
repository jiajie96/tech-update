# AI Agents Weekly Update — May 13, 2026

## Highlights

- **SAP Unveils the Autonomous Enterprise at Sapphire 2026**: SAP announced a unified AI platform deploying 50+ domain-specific Joule Assistants orchestrating 200+ specialized agents across finance, supply chain, procurement, and HR — plus a new vendor-agnostic AI Agent Hub for governing agents across the enterprise.
- **AWS AgentCore Payments Goes Live**: Amazon Bedrock AgentCore Payments launched in preview on May 7, built with Coinbase and Stripe, enabling AI agents to autonomously pay for APIs and services using stablecoin micropayments.
- **Five Eyes Issues Urgent Agentic AI Guidance**: Security agencies from the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand released joint guidance warning that rapid agentic AI rollouts carry significant risk, recommending slow deployment starting with low-risk tasks.

## Detailed Updates

### SAP Sapphire 2026: The Autonomous Enterprise
At SAP Sapphire 2026, CEO Christian Klein unveiled the Autonomous Enterprise vision — a unified AI platform for building, contextualizing, and governing agents alongside an autonomous suite for core business operations. The platform will deploy more than 50 domain-specific Joule Assistants across finance, supply chain, procurement, HCM, and customer experience, orchestrating over 200 specialized agents. SAP also opened the AI Agent Hub, a vendor-agnostic command center for inventorying and governing AI agents, LLMs, and MCP servers regardless of vendor.

### SAP and NVIDIA Expand Agent Security Collaboration
SAP and NVIDIA deepened their partnership to bring trust and governance to specialized enterprise AI agents. The collaboration focuses on enabling enterprises to run domain-specific agents with built-in security and governance controls across finance, procurement, supply chain, and manufacturing — where high-stakes business decisions are made.

### AWS AgentCore Payments: Agents That Pay for Themselves
Amazon Bedrock AgentCore Payments launched in preview on May 7, 2026, built in partnership with Coinbase and Stripe. The system allows AI agents to autonomously pay for APIs, MCP servers, and paywalled content using stablecoin micropayments, introducing a new paradigm for agent economic autonomy. This could fundamentally change how agent-based services are monetized and consumed.

### Five Eyes Agencies Warn on Agentic AI Risks
Security agencies from the Five Eyes alliance (US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand) released urgent joint guidance warning organizations that rapid deployments of agentic AI are too risky. The agencies recommend deploying AI agents slowly and carefully, starting with low-risk tasks and keeping humans firmly in the loop as oversight mechanisms.

### AI Agents Control 30% of DeFi Liquidity
A report released on May 12 reveals that autonomous AI agents — rather than human traders — now control approximately 30% of the total value locked in top-tier liquidity pools across Solana and Ethereum. This milestone highlights the growing intersection of agentic AI and decentralized finance, raising both efficiency and governance questions.

### Enterprise Agent Adoption: Wide but Shallow
New industry data shows 79% of companies have adopted AI agents in some capacity, but only 2% have reached full deployment. Meanwhile, 55% of leaders express concern about agent reliability and error rates, suggesting that governance and trust remain the primary barriers to scaling.

### Grafana Shifts from Dashboards to Autonomous Agents
Grafana announced a strategic pivot from its traditional dashboard-centric approach toward autonomous AI agents for observability. The shift signals that even infrastructure tooling companies are embracing agentic architectures to automate monitoring, alerting, and incident response workflows.

### Salesforce, Cloudflare, and Microsoft Activate Agent Platforms
Three major platforms simultaneously activated autonomous agent capabilities: Salesforce opened its system for agents to execute workflows directly, Cloudflare enabled agents to deploy applications independently, and Microsoft launched Agent 365 to automate enterprise workflows — marking a wave of enterprise agent infrastructure going live.
