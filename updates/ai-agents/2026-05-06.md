# AI Agents Weekly Update — May 6, 2026

## Highlights

- **ServiceNow unveils Autonomous Workforce at Knowledge 2026**: A suite of AI "specialists" that complete entire business processes without human intervention, with partners reporting 90-99% automation rates on IT service desk cases.
- **MCP crosses 164 million monthly downloads**: The Model Context Protocol has become the de facto standard for AI-tool integration, with every major AI provider now adopting it under the Linux Foundation's Agentic AI Foundation.
- **bajji launches AvatarBook trust protocol**: A new settlement protocol enabling AI agents to autonomously place orders, fulfill tasks, and settle payments without human approval at every step.

## Detailed Updates

### ServiceNow and NVIDIA Partner on Enterprise Autonomous Agents

At ServiceNow Knowledge 2026 (May 5-7), ServiceNow unveiled a major expansion of its Autonomous Workforce — AI "specialists" that don't just assist human workers but complete entire business processes from start to finish. The new AI specialists span IT operations, CRM, HR, finance, legal, procurement, and security. Early results are striking: ServiceNow's internal AI specialist resolves IT cases 99% faster than human agents, Docusign targets 90% autonomous resolution of IT tickets, Honeywell reports eliminating the majority of service desk conversations, and the city of Raleigh achieves a 98% deflection rate on employee requests. NVIDIA is extending its collaboration to deliver governed autonomous agents from employee desktops to AI factories.

### MCP Protocol Reaches Unprecedented Adoption

The Model Context Protocol (MCP) has crossed 164 million monthly downloads on PyPI alone by April 2026. The Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF), launched in December 2025 with six co-founders — OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, AWS, and Block — has grown to nearly 150 member organizations, making it one of the fastest-growing foundations in Linux Foundation history. The industry is converging on a three-layer protocol stack: MCP for agent-to-tool communication, A2A (Agent-to-Agent by Google) for inter-agent collaboration, and WebMCP for web access.

### bajji Launches AvatarBook Settlement Protocol

bajji Inc. announced AvatarBook, a Trust & Settlement Protocol enabling AI agents to autonomously place orders, fulfill them, and settle payments without human approval at each step. During its public beta, 28 agents went live executing over 2,300 skill transactions, with more than half of those agents created by external builders. This represents a key step in building the infrastructure needed for autonomous agent economies.

### Gartner Predicts 40% Enterprise Agent Adoption — With Caveats

According to Gartner, 40% of enterprise applications will include embedded, task-specific AI agents by the end of 2026. However, the analyst firm also warns that 40% of agentic AI projects are at risk of failure by 2027 due to messy governance and unclear ROI. The challenge has shifted from "can agents work?" to "can organizations govern them?"

### Business Tools Embrace Agent Integration

Major business platforms including Notion, Slack, Linear, GitHub, Atlassian, HubSpot, Salesforce, and Stripe have all released MCP servers or announced official support. Developer frameworks LangChain, LlamaIndex, and others have added native MCP support, making it straightforward to connect agent systems to enterprise tools.

### Google's A2A Protocol Complements MCP

Google's Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol, donated to the Linux Foundation in June 2025, standardizes how AI agents discover, communicate, and collaborate with each other regardless of underlying framework. While MCP handles agent-to-tool communication, A2A functions as "HTTP for AI agents," providing universal inter-agent communication.
