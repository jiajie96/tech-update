# AI Agents Weekly Update — April 28, 2026

## Highlights

- The Linux Foundation launched the Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF), unifying key open standards including MCP, goose, and AGENTS.md under one governance umbrella.
- Google released Workspace Studio, a no-code platform for building AI agents across Gmail, Docs, and Sheets.
- Stanford AI Index reports agent success on real-world computer tasks jumped from 12% to 66% year over year.
- OWASP published its Top 10 for Agentic Applications, marking a milestone in formal security guidance for agent-based systems.

---

## Detailed Updates

### Agentic AI Foundation Takes Shape

The Linux Foundation officially announced the Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF), a new umbrella organization designed to accelerate the development of open, interoperable agent infrastructure. The foundation is anchored by three pillars: MCP (Model Context Protocol), goose, and the AGENTS.md specification. MCP alone has reached impressive scale — over 10,000 published servers, more than 97 million SDK downloads, and adoption across major platforms including Claude, Cursor, Copilot, Gemini, VS Code, and ChatGPT. The MCP Dev Summit held in New York City on April 2-3 drew approximately 1,200 attendees, signaling strong community momentum. Meanwhile, AGENTS.md has been adopted by over 60,000 open-source projects, establishing it as a de facto standard for declaring agent capabilities in codebases.

### Enterprise Adoption Accelerating — With Growing Pains

Enterprise adoption of AI agents continues its steep climb. Analysts now project that 40% of enterprise applications will incorporate AI agents by year-end 2026, a dramatic leap from just 5% in 2025. However, the speed of adoption is outpacing organizational readiness: while 96% of organizations report using AI agents in some capacity, 94% express concern about agent sprawl — the proliferation of uncoordinated agents across teams and systems.

On the partnership front, Microsoft and MYOB announced a five-year collaboration to embed AI agents into small business tools, targeting accounting, invoicing, and compliance workflows. Microsoft also released the Agent Governance Toolkit, an open-source runtime security framework designed to help enterprises manage agent permissions, audit trails, and policy enforcement at scale.

### Platform Updates Across the Big Three

Google rolled out Workspace Studio on April 22, offering a no-code platform that lets business users build AI agents operating across Gmail, Docs, and Sheets without writing a line of code. Separately, Google Cloud introduced the Data Agents Kit, which brings "agentic skills" directly into developer tooling for data pipelines and analytics workflows.

Amazon doubled down on MCP within the AWS ecosystem, deepening native integration points for agent orchestration across its cloud services. The move positions AWS as a first-class environment for MCP-based agent deployments alongside Google Cloud and Azure.

GPT-5.5 is being positioned as a unified "super app" that combines conversational AI, coding tools, and a built-in browser into a single agent-capable interface, blurring the line between assistant and autonomous agent.

### Performance Gains Become Hard to Ignore

The 2026 Stanford AI Index delivered a striking data point: AI agents jumped from a 12% success rate to 66% on real-world computer tasks in just one year. While far from perfect, this trajectory suggests that agent reliability is improving faster than many predicted.

On the enterprise operations side, Automation Anywhere reported that AI agents now auto-resolve more than 80% of IT support requests in production deployments, cutting support costs by roughly 50%. These numbers are pushing more organizations to move agents from pilot programs into full production.

### Governance Struggles to Keep Pace

OWASP published its Top 10 for Agentic Applications for 2026, providing the first widely recognized security framework specifically tailored to agent architectures. The list addresses risks including excessive autonomy, insecure tool use, and inadequate human-in-the-loop controls.

Despite these efforts, the broader picture remains concerning. Enterprises are moving agents from testing to production faster than governance systems can keep up. The gap between deployment velocity and oversight maturity is emerging as one of the defining challenges of 2026.
