# Web3 Weekly Update — May 9, 2026

## Highlights

- **$292M Kelp DAO Hack Sets 2026 DeFi Record**: A $292 million exploit of Kelp DAO became the largest DeFi hack of 2026, surpassing the $285 million Drift exploit from April 1. The incident reignites debate over smart contract security in restaking protocols.
- **SEC Chair Signals New Rules for Onchain Markets**: The SEC chair indicated upcoming regulations for onchain markets, connecting the rise of AI-powered financial systems with growing demand for blockchain-based market infrastructure and automated settlement.
- **Kraken Applies for Federal Bank Charter**: Kraken's parent company Payward filed for an OCC charter to become a federal crypto bank, adding to its existing Wyoming bank charter and Fed master account.

## Detailed Updates

### Kelp DAO $292M Exploit
A $292 million hack of Kelp DAO set the record for the largest DeFi exploit in 2026, following the $285 million Drift exploit on April 1. The attack underscores persistent security challenges in the restaking and liquid staking protocol space, where complex smart contract interactions create expanded attack surfaces. The DeFi community is calling for more rigorous auditing standards and insurance mechanisms.

### SEC Onchain Market Regulation
The SEC chair signaled new rules for onchain markets, drawing explicit connections between AI-powered financial systems and the growing demand for blockchain-based market infrastructure. The regulatory direction suggests upcoming frameworks that would govern automated settlement systems and decentralized market making, potentially bringing greater institutional clarity to DeFi operations.

### Kraken's Federal Banking Ambitions
Kraken's parent company Payward applied for an OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency) charter in a bid to become a federally regulated crypto bank. This would complement Kraken's existing Wyoming bank charter and Federal Reserve master account, positioning the exchange as one of the most comprehensively regulated crypto businesses in the United States.

### Stablecoin Regulation and the CLARITY Act
The CLARITY Act, a US crypto market structure bill, includes provisions that would restrict crypto companies from offering bank-like interest or yield simply for holding stablecoins, though the text reportedly still permits rewards tied to actual platform activity. ECB President Lagarde separately warned that large stablecoins like Tether and USDC, which now dominate a $310 billion market, pose financial stability risks.

### Consensus Miami 2026 Takeaways
Executives from MoonPay, Ripple, and Paxos shared at Consensus Miami 2026 that regulation has accelerated stablecoin adoption, but infrastructure gaps, privacy challenges, and distribution hurdles remain. The conference highlighted the growing intersection of AI agents and crypto user experience.

### Chainlink CCIP Hits $1.3B Weekly Volume
Chainlink's Cross-Chain Interoperability Protocol (CCIP) is processing over $1.3 billion in weekly volume, demonstrating strong growth in cross-chain infrastructure adoption. The protocol serves as critical plumbing for multi-chain DeFi operations and institutional cross-chain transfers.

### Polkadot Hard Supply Cap and JAM Upgrade
Polkadot introduced a hard supply cap and reduced token issuance in May 2026, improving its long-term tokenomics. The upcoming JAM upgrade aims to transform Polkadot into a decentralized compute platform, expanding its utility beyond cross-chain messaging.

### BNY Mellon Digital Asset Custody in Abu Dhabi
BNY Mellon, with $59 trillion in assets under custody, is launching digital asset custody services in Abu Dhabi's ADGM financial center. The service initially supports Bitcoin and Ethereum for institutional clients, representing another milestone in traditional finance's integration with crypto.

### Helium Mobile Crosses 160K Subscribers
Helium's decentralized mobile network crossed 160,000 subscribers and is generating real revenue, standing out as one of the few Web3 projects with significant real-world usage and a sustainable business model in 2026.
