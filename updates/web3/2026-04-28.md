## Detailed Updates

### Kelp DAO Exploit Aftermath and Recovery (April 18-27)

The $292 million Kelp DAO hack — the largest DeFi exploit of 2026 — continued to dominate Web3 news this week. On April 18, attackers exploited the protocol's 1-of-1 verifier configuration for cross-chain LayerZero messages. By DDoS-attacking RPC nodes and forcing failover to attacker-controlled nodes, the Lazarus Group (attributed by Chainalysis and LayerZero) injected fraudulent messages that released ~116,500 rsETH across 20 chains. The contagion was severe: $13.2 billion in TVL exited 20+ protocols within 48 hours. Aave, SparkLend, and Fluid froze rsETH markets immediately. On April 21, Arbitrum's Network Security Council froze $71 million in attacker funds. By April 27, Aave was spearheading a coordinated recovery effort with $300M+ in pledged support from Consensys, Lido, EtherFi, and others — an unprecedented level of industry coordination.

### Strategy Adds 3,273 BTC (April 27)

Michael Saylor's Strategy acquired 3,273 Bitcoin for approximately $255 million at an average price of $77,906 per BTC, announced April 27. This brings total holdings to 818,334 BTC, acquired at a cumulative cost of $61.81 billion ($75,537 average). The pace of accumulation slowed compared to recent weeks, partly due to a slowdown in sales of STRC perpetual preferred shares, which fund the purchases.

### HashKey Agent Economy Paper (April 21)

Dr. Xiao Feng of HashKey Group published "On-Chain Finance and Tokenization in the Era of the Agent Economy" on April 21, proposing frameworks for integrating AI agents with blockchain tokenization. The paper outlines how autonomous AI agents could manage portfolios, execute on-chain transactions, and interact with tokenized assets — bridging the AI agent boom with Web3 infrastructure.

### MiCA Stablecoin Developments

Banking Circle, now licensed under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) framework, joined the bank stablecoin settlement race in Europe. This adds to the broader trend of institutional stablecoin adoption — the sector hit a historic $320 billion in total market value. Separately, Western Union continues exploring a stablecoin launch for SWIFT-free global settlement, though no firm timeline has been announced.
