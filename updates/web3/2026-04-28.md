# Web3 Weekly Update — April 28, 2026

## Highlights

- **$292M Kelp DAO Hack Rocks DeFi**: The largest DeFi exploit of 2026 exploited a compromised bridge verifier, draining wrapped ether across 20 chains and triggering a $13 billion TVL exodus.
- **Aave Spearheads $300M Recovery Effort**: Consensys, Lido, EtherFi, and others rally behind a coordinated rescue plan for affected users.
- **Solana's Alpenglow Upgrade Targets 100ms Finality**: A full consensus rewrite replacing Proof of History aims to make transaction irreversibility near-instant.

## Detailed Updates

### Kelp DAO Exploit: $292 Million Stolen

On April 18, Kelp DAO — a liquid restaking protocol — suffered the largest DeFi hack of 2026. Attackers exploited the protocol's 1-of-1 verifier configuration for cross-chain LayerZero messages. By compromising remote procedure call (RPC) nodes and launching a DDoS attack to force failover to attacker-controlled nodes, they injected malicious messages that tricked the verifier into releasing funds. Wrapped ether was drained across 20 chains. Cybersecurity firms have attributed the attack to the North Korea-linked Lazarus Group. The incident triggered massive market disruption, with over $13 billion in TVL exiting various platforms in the following 48 hours.

### Aave-Led Recovery Effort

In response to the Kelp DAO fallout, Aave is spearheading a coordinated DeFi recovery effort with more than $300 million in pledged support from major players including Consensys, Lido, EtherFi, and others. Several platforms including Aave, SparkLend, and Fluid froze their rsETH markets immediately after the exploit to prevent accumulating additional bad debt. The recovery effort represents an unprecedented level of industry coordination and raises questions about DeFi's informal safety nets.

### Solana Alpenglow Consensus Rewrite

Solana's 2026 roadmap centers on Alpenglow, a complete rewrite of its consensus and block propagation layers targeted for early to mid-2026. Alpenglow replaces both Proof of History and Tower BFT with two new components: Votor for consensus voting and Rotor for block propagation. The upgrade promises to reduce finality to 100-150 milliseconds, making transaction irreversibility near-instant. A separate March upgrade made token operations up to 98% more efficient, significantly reducing transaction fees.

### Ethereum Glamsterdam Upgrade Approaching

Ethereum's 2026 roadmap features two major upgrades: Glamsterdam in the first half and Hegota in the second. Glamsterdam focuses on execution efficiency and enshrining proposer-builder separation at the protocol level, while Hegota will address state growth, node sustainability, and censorship resistance. Both upgrades reflect Ethereum's shift toward operational sustainability and decentralization hardening.

### Stablecoin Market Hits $320 Billion

The stablecoin sector reached a historic $320 billion in total market value, cementing its role as core DeFi infrastructure. Western Union is now eyeing a stablecoin launch to settle global transactions without SWIFT, signaling growing institutional appetite for blockchain-based payment rails. The move would represent one of the largest traditional financial institutions to enter the stablecoin market directly.

### HashKey Tokenization and Agent Economy

Dr. Xiao Feng of HashKey Group unveiled a paper on April 21 introducing frameworks for integrating AI agents with blockchain tokenization. The "On-Chain Finance and Tokenization in the Era of the Agent Economy" paper outlines how AI agents could autonomously interact with tokenized assets, manage portfolios, and execute on-chain transactions — a vision that bridges the AI agent boom with Web3 infrastructure.

### Web3 Market Crosses $68 Billion

The Web3 market size crossed $68 billion in early 2026, with analysts projecting it could surpass $400 billion by decade's end. Hong Kong emerged as a hub for Web3 and crypto innovation this month, hosting major conferences that brought together institutional investors, developers, and regulators exploring decentralized finance, tokenization, and AI-powered economic models.

### Additional DeFi Exploits

Beyond the Kelp DAO hack, April saw multiple security incidents: Volo Protocol on Sui was drained of $3.5 million through a smart contract vulnerability, and CoW Swap users lost approximately $1.2 million after an attacker social-engineered the domain registrar. The string of exploits has intensified calls for stronger DeFi security standards and formal verification of bridge protocols.
