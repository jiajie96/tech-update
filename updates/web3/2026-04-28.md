- **GENIUS Act and CLARITY Act advance through Congress**, establishing the first comprehensive federal frameworks for stablecoin issuance and crypto asset jurisdiction in the United States.

## Detailed Updates

### Regulation & Policy

The U.S. regulatory landscape for digital assets saw significant movement this month. On April 13, the SEC issued new guidance on broker-dealer registration requirements for platforms offering crypto asset security interfaces, signaling a more structured approach to enforcement.

Two landmark bills are now progressing through Congress. The **GENIUS Act** establishes a federal regime governing stablecoin issuance, covering reserve requirements, audit standards, and regulatory oversight. Meanwhile, the **CLARITY Act** passed the House, drawing a jurisdictional line between the SEC and CFTC over different classes of digital assets. Galaxy Digital CEO Mike Novogratz expressed confidence that the CLARITY Act will be finalized in May.

On the corporate side, Western Union is reportedly exploring a stablecoin launch aimed at enabling global transactions that bypass SWIFT infrastructure — a move that could bring Web3 payment rails to traditional remittance corridors.

### Hong Kong Web3 Festival & AI-Blockchain Convergence

Hong Kong's Web3 Festival served as a hub for institutional and technical announcements. Key events included RWA Demo Day, Crypto 2026, Cypher Asia, the HSC Asset Management Forum, and HashKey's On-Chain Summit.

A notable announcement came on April 14 when **Zetrix AI and CAICT unveiled Avatar**, a blockchain-based trust protocol designed for AI agents, presented at the World Internet Conference Asia Pacific. The protocol aims to provide verifiable identity and trust infrastructure as autonomous agents become more prevalent in on-chain environments.

On April 21, Dr. Xiao Feng of HashKey published a paper on **On-Chain Finance and Tokenization in the Agent Economy**, outlining how tokenized assets and programmable finance could underpin the next wave of AI-driven economic activity. The intersection of AI agents and blockchain trust layers is emerging as a defining theme for 2026.

### Market & Funding

The broader Web3 market crossed **$68 billion** in early 2026, with projections placing it above $400 billion by the end of the decade. During the week of April 6–12, the sector recorded 14 funding deals totaling $77.7 million, led by **Pharos' $44 million Series A**.

Michael Saylor's Strategy continued its accumulation strategy, purchasing 3,273 BTC for $255 million between April 20 and 26. The firm's total Bitcoin holdings now stand at 818,334 BTC. However, analysts note that Bitcoin's recent rally is showing signs of fatigue, suggesting a potential consolidation period ahead.

### DeFi Security

Security remains a persistent concern. A massive crypto hack prompted a rescue plan involving hundreds of millions of dollars to protect Aave users from losses. Separately, **Volo**, a protocol on the Sui network, suffered a $3.5 million exploit. These incidents underscore the ongoing need for stronger audit practices and risk management across DeFi.
