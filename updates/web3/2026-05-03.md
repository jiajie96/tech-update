# Web3 Weekly Update — May 3, 2026

## Highlights

- **Clarity Act text released with stablecoin yield rules**: The compromise legislation allows crypto firms to offer stablecoin rewards based on "bona fide activities" while blocking bank-deposit-like yield offerings.
- **Ethereum validators finalize block 25 million**: A milestone reached on May 1 after nearly 11 years of network operation under proof-of-stake consensus.
- **Consensys pushes for explicit DeFi protections**: The Ethereum ecosystem company calls for regulatory clarity distinguishing user-generated DeFi yields from issuer-provided returns.

## Detailed Updates

### Clarity Act Stablecoin Provisions Draw Industry Attention
The Clarity Act compromise text, released in early May 2026, establishes rules for stablecoin yield offerings. Crypto firms are prohibited from offering yield on stablecoin deposits that functionally resemble bank interest. However, incentives based on "bona fide activities or bona fide transactions" — similar to credit card rewards — are permitted. Senator Cynthia Lummis indicated that stablecoin and market structure provisions are "almost 99% sorted out," with a possible Senate Banking Committee markup as early as the week of May 11.

### Consensys Calls for DeFi Regulatory Carve-Outs
Consensys filed comments urging regulators to explicitly protect decentralized finance activities from stablecoin yield restrictions, arguing that user-generated yields in DeFi protocols differ fundamentally from issuer-provided returns. The call highlights growing industry concern that broad regulatory language could inadvertently capture permissionless DeFi protocols alongside centralized issuers.

### Ethereum Hits Block 25 Million Milestone
Ethereum validators finalized block 25,000,000 on May 1, 2026, a milestone reached without any protocol changes. The network continues executing its 2026 upgrade roadmap, with the Glamsterdam upgrade — focused on gas optimizations and Enshrined Proposer-Builder Separation (ePBS) — targeted for the first half of 2026, followed by the Hegotá upgrade later in the year.

### GENIUS Act Implementation Progresses
Treasury and regulatory bodies are working on implementing the GENIUS Act by its January 18, 2027 deadline. The act's core requirement mandates that payment stablecoins be fully backed by high-quality liquid assets, with monthly independent attestations. The implementation process is shaping how compliant stablecoin issuers will operate in the US market.

### SBI Holdings Expands Crypto Presence
Japan's SBI Holdings is expanding its crypto ambitions with new plans in Singapore and a Visa partnership for bank cards that allow users to accumulate digital assets. The move represents continued institutional interest in crypto infrastructure across Asia.

### Web3 Market Crosses $68 Billion
The Web3 market size has crossed the $68 billion mark in early 2026, with analysts projecting it could surpass $400 billion by the end of the decade. Total value locked in Web3 protocols has surged, making wallet security a top priority for retail and institutional participants alike.

### Emerging Protocols Reshape Infrastructure
Five protocols are shaping Web3's next phase: EigenLayer (security economics via restaking), Celestia (modular blockchain architecture), LayerZero (cross-chain messaging), Lens Protocol (decentralized social data ownership), and ERC-4337 (account abstraction for mainstream UX). Each addresses distinct infrastructure challenges needed for broader adoption.
