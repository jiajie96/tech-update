# Web3 Weekly Update — May 14, 2026

## Highlights

- **Senate CLARITY Act Markup Vote Today**: The U.S. Senate Banking Committee holds a critical markup vote on the 309-page Digital Asset Market CLARITY Act, which would codify Bitcoin and Ether as digital commodities under federal law, with Citi analysts tying a $143K BTC price target to passage.
- **40+ DeFi Protocols Shut Down in 2026 Amid $770M Hack Crisis**: A wave of protocol closures continues as $770M+ has been stolen in DeFi hacks year-to-date, with the $293M Kelp DAO exploit and $285M Drift Protocol hack accounting for nearly 88% of April losses.
- **Code4rena Announces Wind Down**: The prominent Web3 competitive auditing platform Code4rena announced it will wind down operations, marking the end of one of crypto's most recognized security communities.

## Detailed Updates

### CLARITY Act Vote — A Defining Moment for U.S. Crypto
The U.S. Senate Banking Committee holds a critical markup vote today (May 14) at 10:30 AM ET on the full 309-page Digital Asset Market CLARITY Act. The bill would formally classify Bitcoin and Ether as digital commodities under CFTC jurisdiction, codifying the March 2026 joint SEC-CFTC guidance into federal statute that no future administration could reverse with a regulatory memo. Polymarket puts odds of passage in 2026 at 62%, and Citi analysts project $15 billion in additional net ETF inflows upon passage. US spot Bitcoin ETFs have already attracted nearly $1 billion in inflows in early May.

### Kelp DAO $293M Exploit Fallout
The aftermath of the Kelp DAO exploit continues to ripple through DeFi. The attacker forged a fake LayerZero cross-chain message, tricking the liquid restaking protocol into releasing 116,500 rsETH — roughly 18% of the circulating supply — across 20+ networks including Arbitrum, Base, Linea, and Scroll. The attack has been attributed to North Korea's Lazarus Group. The exploit triggered $1.3 billion in withdrawals and has intensified scrutiny of cross-chain bridge security.

### DeFi Protocol Shutdown Wave
Over 40 DeFi protocols have ceased operations or moved to wind-down mode between January and early May 2026. With $770M+ stolen in hacks year-to-date, smaller protocols face existential questions about the cost of security versus the viability of their business models. The two largest exploits — Kelp DAO ($293M) and Drift Protocol ($285M) — accounted for the overwhelming majority of April's total losses.

### Code4rena Winds Down Operations
Web3 security platform Code4rena officially announced plans to wind down, ending one of crypto's most recognized competitive auditing communities. The platform confirmed all ongoing security contests, audits, and bounty programs will be completed before closure. Code4rena played a significant role in the DeFi security ecosystem by crowdsourcing smart contract audits through competitive bounties.

### Transit Finance Drained of $1.88M
In a separate incident on May 13, cross-chain DEX aggregator Transit Finance was drained of $1.88M in DAI, with the stolen funds sitting in a fresh Ethereum wallet. The exploit adds to a growing list of cross-chain bridge and aggregator vulnerabilities being targeted in 2026.

### Chainlink CCIP Processes $1.3B Weekly Volume
Chainlink's Cross-Chain Interoperability Protocol (CCIP) continues to show strong momentum, processing over $1.3 billion in weekly transaction volume in May 2026. The protocol's growth reflects increasing demand for secure cross-chain infrastructure, particularly in the wake of bridge exploits that have highlighted the risks of less battle-tested interoperability solutions.

### Polkadot JAM Upgrade and Supply Cap
Polkadot introduced a hard supply cap and reduced token issuance in a major tokenomics change. The upcoming JAM upgrade aims to transform Polkadot into a decentralized compute platform, moving beyond its original relay chain architecture to compete with general-purpose blockchain computing platforms.

### Japan Unlocks Institutional Crypto Capital
Japan completed a revised crypto asset regulatory framework in late 2025 that has now unlocked institutional capital from major financial institutions. The FSA approved stablecoin issuance by licensed banks, making Japan one of the first major economies to formalize bank-issued stablecoins. The Asia-Pacific region remains the fastest-growing region for Web3 adoption in 2026.
