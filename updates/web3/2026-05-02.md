## Detailed Updates

### Kelp DAO Exploit: Largest DeFi Hack of 2026
On April 18, a forged LayerZero message drained 116,500 rsETH ($292 million) from Kelp DAO's cross-chain bridge. Chainalysis confirmed North Korea's Lazarus Group as the attacker. The root cause was a 1-of-1 verifier configuration — the attackers compromised RPC nodes used for message verification, injected malicious messages, and DDoS'd backup nodes to force failover to attacker-controlled infrastructure. The fallout was severe: Aave saw $8.45 billion in deposit exits within 48 hours, driving a $13.21 billion slide in total DeFi TVL. Aave has since raised $160 million toward the $200 million needed to cover bad debt, with Mantle and AAVE DAO contributing $127 million.

### U.S. Crypto Regulation Advances
Multiple regulatory milestones are converging in May. The SEC will host a roundtable on the CLARITY Act, which aims to provide clearer classification rules for digital assets. The Senate Banking Committee is targeting a markup session the week of May 11. Analysts are divided on passage odds — Galaxy Digital's Alex Thorn puts it at 50/50, while TD Cowen's Jaret Seiberg estimates one-third. Gemini received a Derivatives Clearing Organization (DCO) license from the CFTC, allowing it to serve as a clearinghouse for regulated derivatives and prediction markets.

### Bitcoin Range-Bound as Market Digests Macro Signals
Bitcoin held $75,000 support and ticked up to $77,000, remaining range-bound as traders maintain a short bias. On April 30, Bitcoin spot ETFs recorded a net inflow of $14.76 million — the first positive flow after three consecutive days of outflows. The crypto market broadly slid 1–4% on the week, with XRP taking the largest hit, as big tech earnings provided mixed signals on risk appetite.

### Institutional and TradFi Convergence
The convergence of traditional finance and crypto infrastructure continues to accelerate. SBI Holdings is eyeing a stake in crypto exchange Bitbank and pursuing plans in Singapore, plus a Visa partnership for bank cards that accumulate digital assets. Ondo Finance partnered with Ondo Global Markets for tokenized U.S. stocks and ETFs, exemplifying how tokenized real-world assets are bridging the gap between crypto infrastructure and traditional securities exposure. PayPal restructured into three core units, with crypto and payments now operating as a standalone division.

### Hong Kong Fintech 2030 Vision
Hong Kong unveiled its Fintech 2030 strategy, prioritizing stablecoin regulation and global regulatory coordination. The initiative positions Hong Kong as a competing hub to Singapore and Dubai for digital asset businesses, with a focus on creating clear licensing pathways and sandbox environments for stablecoin issuers.

The conference comes at a critical moment as the industry navigates post-hack security concerns alongside growing institutional interest.
