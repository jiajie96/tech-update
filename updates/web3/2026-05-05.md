# Web3 Weekly Update — May 5, 2026

## Highlights

- **Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana officially classified as digital commodities**: Under new U.S. regulation, 15+ digital assets are now regulated by the CFTC rather than the SEC, providing long-sought regulatory clarity.
- **DTCC to launch tokenized securities platform**: Wall Street's clearing giant plans a July pilot and October full launch for tokenized securities, marking a major step in TradFi-DeFi convergence.
- **Consensus 2026 kicks off in Miami**: The industry's flagship conference (May 5-7) focuses on stablecoins, tokenization, and TradFi/DeFi integration.

## Detailed Updates

### U.S. Regulatory Clarity: Digital Commodities Classification

In a watershed moment for the crypto industry, Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, XRP, and 12 other digital assets have been officially classified as digital commodities under U.S. law, regulated by the CFTC rather than the SEC. The GENIUS Act — the federal stablecoin law signed by President Trump in July 2025 — is moving toward full implementation by July 18, 2026, with the FDIC already proposing procedures to allow banks to issue stablecoins.

### CLARITY Act Progress Stalls

The CLARITY Act markup has slipped from April to May, with passage odds dropping. Galaxy Digital's Alex Thorn puts May passage odds at 50/50, while TD Cowen's Jaret Seiberg estimates one-third probability. Polymarket's passage odds dropped from 64% to 47%. The stablecoin yield compromise is seen as the key factor that could open a path to passing broader digital asset regulation.

### DTCC Tokenized Securities Platform

The DTCC, Wall Street's clearing infrastructure backbone, announced plans to launch a tokenized securities platform with a July pilot and October full launch. This represents one of the most significant institutional moves toward on-chain securities, potentially bringing trillions in traditional assets to blockchain rails.

### Ethereum Glamsterdam Upgrade Expected H1 2026

Ethereum's Glamsterdam upgrade, which enhances L1 scalability and decentralization, is expected before the end of H1 2026. Analysts suggest the upgrade could boost ETH momentum and push prices toward $3,000 — approximately 30% upside from current levels. Ethereum treasury purchases of $238 million have signaled institutional confidence.

### Solana Ecosystem Growth

Solana received clearer regulatory designation in the U.S., while real-world asset (RWA) activity on the network reached new highs across holders, value, and lending. The Solana Foundation secured a partnership with Shinhan Card, opening stablecoin transactions to over 28 million cardholders. Coinbase is also boosting Solana trading through DFlow integration to reduce trade failures.

### Account Abstraction Eliminates Seed Phrases

Account abstraction has eliminated seed phrases in 2026, making crypto wallets as easy to use as email accounts. Combined with gas sponsorship (where dApp developers subsidize transaction fees), these UX improvements are removing longstanding barriers to mainstream adoption.

### Real-World Asset Tokenization Continues to Surge

RWA tokenization remains one of the strongest Web3 trends, with real estate, commodities, and financial instruments increasingly represented on-chain for fractional ownership. Japan's revised crypto asset regulatory framework (completed late 2025) has made it one of the world's highest-growth DeFi markets in 2026.

### Consensus Miami 2026

Consensus 2026 runs May 5-7 in Miami, with the agenda covering stablecoins, tokenization, TradFi & DeFi convergence, and the regulatory landscape. The conference comes at a pivotal moment as institutional adoption accelerates and regulatory frameworks solidify globally.
