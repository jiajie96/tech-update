# Web3 Weekly Update — May 13, 2026

## Highlights

- **CLARITY Act Senate Vote Scheduled for May 14**: The Senate Banking Committee has officially scheduled a markup session for the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act, with Polymarket traders assigning a 75% probability it becomes law in 2026.
- **Kelp DAO Exploit Sets New 2026 DeFi Hack Record**: A major exploit of Kelp DAO surpassed the $285 million Drift Protocol hack from April 1 as the largest DeFi security breach of the year, prompting Aave, Lido Finance, and Ethena to pause affected markets.
- **Kraken Parent and Franklin Templeton Partner on Blockchain Finance**: Payward (Kraken's parent company) and Franklin Templeton announced a strategic collaboration to move mainstream financial products onto blockchain networks.

## Detailed Updates

### CLARITY Act Heads to Senate Markup
The Senate Banking Committee officially scheduled the markup session for the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act for Thursday, May 14. Senators Thom Tillis and Angela Alsobrooks released a compromise on stablecoin yield provisions, banning yield equivalent to bank deposits while allowing "bona fide activities." Major crypto firms including Coinbase and Circle backed the compromise, and Polymarket prediction markets currently assign a 75% probability the bill becomes law this year.

### Kelp DAO Exploit Rocks DeFi
A Kelp DAO exploit set a new record for the largest DeFi hack of 2026, surpassing the $285 million Drift Protocol breach from April 1. In response, major protocols including Aave, Lido Finance, and Ethena paused markets involving rsETH to contain potential contagion. The incident underscores persistent security challenges in DeFi, particularly around liquid restaking protocols.

### Drift Protocol Hack Aftermath
Analysis continues on the April 1 Drift Protocol exploit, which used Solana's "durable nonces" feature to trick Drift Security Council members into pre-signing transactions that handed over admin control. Chainalysis published a detailed breakdown revealing the attack has been linked to DPRK-affiliated actors. At least 20 protocols across the Solana DeFi ecosystem reported disruptions, pauses, or losses due to their dependency on Drift's liquidity infrastructure.

### Kraken-Franklin Templeton Blockchain Partnership
Payward, the parent company of crypto exchange Kraken, and Franklin Templeton announced a strategic collaboration to bring mainstream financial products onto blockchain networks. The partnership bridges traditional asset management with crypto-native infrastructure, signaling continued institutional convergence with blockchain technology.

### Stablecoin Regulation Goes Global
Seven major economies — the US, EU, UK, Singapore, Hong Kong, UAE, and Japan — now mandate full reserve backing, licensed issuers, and guaranteed redemption rights for stablecoins. This coordinated global regulatory framework treats stablecoins as regulated payment instruments rather than crypto assets, providing clearer compliance pathways for issuers and institutional adopters.

### Chainlink CCIP Processes $1.3B Weekly Volume
Chainlink's Cross-Chain Interoperability Protocol continues showing strong momentum in May 2026, processing over $1.3 billion in weekly cross-chain volume. The protocol's growing adoption reflects increasing demand for secure cross-chain infrastructure as multi-chain DeFi activity expands.

### AI Agents Manage 30% of DeFi Liquidity
A May 12 report reveals that autonomous AI agents now control approximately 30% of the total value locked in top-tier liquidity pools across Solana and Ethereum. This milestone raises new questions about governance, accountability, and systemic risk in algorithmically managed DeFi markets.

### Helium Mobile Crosses 160K Subscribers
Helium's decentralized mobile network crossed 160,000 subscribers and continues generating real revenue, standing out as one of the few Web3 infrastructure projects with genuine consumer adoption. The milestone demonstrates that decentralized physical infrastructure networks (DePIN) can achieve meaningful scale in real-world utility markets.

### Ethereum Protocol Roadmap Update
Ethereum's 2026 development roadmap centers on two upgrades: Glamsterdam (first half) focusing on execution efficiency and proposer-builder separation, and Hegota (second half) addressing state growth, node sustainability, and censorship resistance. Both upgrades aim to strengthen Ethereum's position as the primary smart contract platform.
