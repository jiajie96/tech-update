# Web3 Weekly Update — April 27, 2026

## Highlights

- **KelpDAO $292M exploit becomes largest DeFi hack of 2026**: Attacker minted unbacked rsETH tokens, borrowed ether from Aave; community races to cover $200M shortfall across 20 chains.
- **Bitcoin quantum-proofing race accelerates**: Google researchers say quantum computers could break BTC cryptography in under 9 minutes; BIP 360 and SPHINCS+ signatures in development.
- **DoorDash pays drivers in stablecoins via Stripe's Tempo**: A milestone for stablecoin adoption in mainstream payroll and merchant payments.

## Detailed Updates

### KelpDAO $292M Exploit Rocks DeFi
The largest DeFi hack of 2026 struck KelpDAO, with $292 million drained through a vulnerability that allowed an attacker to create unbacked rsETH tokens and borrow ether from Aave. The exploit left wrapped ether stranded across 20 chains. Aave rallied DeFi partners to contain the fallout, raising nearly 80% of the $200M needed to cover bad debt. Just days later, Volo Protocol on Sui lost another $3.5M in a separate smart-contract exploit — the month's combined DeFi losses exceeded $13 billion.

### Bitcoin Quantum Resistance Push
Google researchers published findings that a sufficiently powerful quantum computer could break Bitcoin's core cryptography in under nine minutes, with such threats potentially emerging by 2029. Developers are pursuing multiple defenses: BIP 360 (removing on-chain public keys), hash-based post-quantum signatures like SPHINCS+, and commit/reveal schemes to shield mempool transactions. Bitcoin's $1.3 trillion market cap makes this the most high-stakes cryptographic migration ever attempted.

### Solana Quantum Readiness
The Solana Foundation is working with Project Eleven to test quantum-resistant cryptography. However, analysis reveals a harsh tradeoff: post-quantum signatures are significantly larger, creating tension between security and Solana's speed-first architecture.

### DoorDash Stablecoin Payments
DoorDash began paying delivery workers in stablecoins via Stripe's Tempo blockchain platform, supporting Ethereum, Solana, and Plasma-issued USDT tokens. This mainstream adoption by a major consumer platform signals stablecoins have crossed from crypto-native speculation to real-world payment infrastructure.

### Ramp $0 Stablecoin Conversions
Peter Thiel-backed unicorn Ramp rolled out zero-fee conversions between USDT and dollars on April 21, supporting multiple chains. Removing conversion fees eliminates a major friction point for stablecoin adoption in business payments.

### HashKey Tokenization & AI Agent Economy
Dr. Xiao Feng, HashKey Group CEO, published a paper on "On-Chain Finance and Tokenization in the Era of the Agent Economy" on April 21. Key advantages highlighted: granularity (splitting assets/permissions into smaller units), automation (smart contract execution), and unified ledger (reducing intermediary steps). Separately, Zetrix and CAICT's Astron launched Avatar, a blockchain trust protocol giving AI agents verified identity and digital asset access.

### Hong Kong Web3 Hub
Hong Kong cemented its position as a global Web3 center with a series of major April events: Hong Kong Web3 Festival, RWA Demo Day, Crypto 2026, Cypher Asia, and the HashKey On-Chain Summit. The concentration of events reflects Hong Kong's regulatory clarity attracting institutional capital.

### Bitcoin ETF Inflows vs. DeFi Jitters
Nearly $1 billion in Bitcoin ETF inflows powered the bull case even as the Kelp hack fueled DeFi anxiety. Bitcoin hit resistance at $80,000 before pulling back to $79,500 as an oil surge triggered broader crypto selloff. Kevin O'Leary pivoted to Bitcoin and Ethereum only, abandoning altcoins.

### Weekly Fundraising
During April 6-12, 14 deals secured $77.7M in disclosed capital, led by Pharos' $44M Series A. Strong early-stage activity across payments, infrastructure, and consumer products signals continued venture confidence.
