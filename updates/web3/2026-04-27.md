# Web3 Weekly Update — April 27, 2026

## Highlights

- **DeFi reels from $13B KelpDAO fallout**: Aave races to cover bad debt, raising ~80% of the $200M needed. The exploit exposes both the fragility and resilience of DeFi governance.
- **DoorDash pays drivers in stablecoins via Stripe's Tempo**: A signal that stablecoins are becoming legitimate business infrastructure, not just crypto-native instruments.
- **Bitcoin BIP 361 proposes quantum-resistant migration**: A three-phase plan to future-proof Bitcoin against quantum computing threats.

## Detailed Updates

### KelpDAO Exploit and DeFi Fallout
DeFi lost $13 billion this month as the KelpDAO exploit sent shockwaves through the ecosystem. Aave's AAVE token dropped 1% as the protocol faced collateral damage, but the community rallied to raise nearly 80% of the $200M needed to cover bad debt. The incident showcases both the worst of DeFi (systemic contagion risk) and the best (community-driven recovery mechanisms).

### DoorDash Stablecoin Payments
DoorDash announced it will pay delivery workers in stablecoins via Stripe's Tempo blockchain platform, supporting Ethereum, Solana, and Plasma-issued USDT tokens. This mainstream adoption by a major consumer platform signals that stablecoins have moved beyond crypto speculation into real-world payment infrastructure.

### Bitcoin Quantum Resistance — BIP 361
Published in April 2026, Bitcoin Improvement Proposal 361 outlines a three-phase migration to quantum-resistant outputs. While quantum computing threats remain theoretical for now, the proactive proposal demonstrates the Bitcoin community's forward-thinking approach to long-term security.

### Solana Alpenglow Consensus Upgrade
Solana plans a major consensus upgrade with the Alpenglow protocol, developed by Anza (a spinoff from Solana Labs). The upgrade introduces Votor (finalizing blocks in 100-150 milliseconds) and Rotor (a more efficient data relay protocol), potentially making Solana the fastest major blockchain by a significant margin.

### HashKey Tokenization & AI Agent Economy
Dr. Xiao Feng, HashKey Group CEO, published a research paper on "On-Chain Finance and Tokenization in the Era of the Agent Economy" on April 21. Separately, Zetrix AI Berhad and CAICT's Astron unveiled Avatar, a blockchain-based trust protocol giving autonomous AI agents verified identity, credentials, and access to digital assets — bridging the AI agent and Web3 worlds.

### Hong Kong Web3 Festival
Hong Kong solidified its position as a global Web3 hub with a series of major events in April, including the Hong Kong Web3 Festival, RWA Demo Day, Crypto 2026, Cypher Asia, and the HashKey On-Chain Summit. The concentration of events reflects Hong Kong's regulatory clarity attracting institutional participation.

### Ramp $0 Stablecoin Conversions
Peter Thiel-backed unicorn Ramp rolled out $0 conversions between USDT and dollars across its product suite on April 21, supporting Ethereum, Solana, and Plasma tokens. Zero-fee conversion removes a major friction point for stablecoin adoption in business payments.

### Kevin O'Leary Goes Bitcoin-Only
On April 22, Kevin O'Leary announced he was pivoting to Bitcoin and Ethereum only, abandoning altcoins and citing the superior scale and survivability of the two largest cryptocurrencies. The move reflects a broader institutional trend toward concentration in blue-chip crypto assets.

### Fundraising Activity
During April 6-12, 14 deals secured $77.7M in disclosed capital, led by Pharos' $44M Series A. Strong early-stage participation across payments, infrastructure, and consumer-facing products suggests continued venture interest despite market turbulence.
