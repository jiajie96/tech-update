# Web3 Weekly Update — April 30, 2026

## Highlights

- **CLARITY Act compromise reached on stablecoin yields**: The long-standing dispute over stablecoin yields in the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act was resolved April 14, with the Senate Banking Committee planning markup in late April.
- **Treasury proposes AML/sanctions framework for stablecoin issuers**: FinCEN and OFAC issued joint rulemaking to implement the GENIUS Act's compliance provisions, with comments due June 9.
- **DeFi security remains brutal**: The Drift exploit on Solana ($285M) and Kelp DAO hack set new records, while CoW Swap lost $1.2M to a domain registrar social engineering attack.

## Detailed Updates

### CLARITY Act Stablecoin Yield Compromise
On April 14, a bipartisan compromise was reached on the contentious stablecoin yield provisions of the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act. The compromise prohibits passive yields from "simply holding" stablecoins but allows rewards tied to payments, transfers, or platform usage. The Senate Banking Committee plans to hold a markup session in late April, potentially clearing the way for the most significant US crypto legislation since the GENIUS Act.

### Treasury AML/CFT Rulemaking for Stablecoin Issuers
On April 8, the US Treasury's FinCEN and OFAC issued a joint Notice of Proposed Rulemaking implementing anti-money laundering and sanctions compliance provisions of the GENIUS Act for payment stablecoin issuers. The proposed rules would require licensed issuers to maintain full AML/CFT programs, file suspicious activity reports, and comply with OFAC sanctions screening. The final rules would become effective 12 months after issuance, with public comments due June 9, 2026.

### Global Stablecoin Regulation Converges
Seven major economies — the US, EU, UK, Singapore, Hong Kong, UAE, and Japan — now mandate full reserve backing, licensed issuers, and guaranteed redemption rights for stablecoins. The stablecoin sector has reached a historic $320 billion in total market value, reflecting growing institutional confidence as regulatory frameworks crystallize. Stablecoins are edging closer to mainstream payroll and merchant payouts.

### Major DeFi Security Incidents
April saw devastating DeFi exploits. The Solana-based Drift perpetual futures exchange was exploited for $285 million through a novel attack using durable nonces to rapidly take over the protocol's Security Council administrative powers, after which the attacker eliminated risk management limits and drained token reserves. The Kelp DAO hack set a new record as the largest DeFi hack of 2026. Additionally, an attacker social-engineered CoW Swap's domain registrar, redirecting visitors to a malicious site and stealing approximately $1.2 million.

### Hong Kong Web3 Festival 2026
Hong Kong hosted Asia's flagship Web3 event with over 50,000 visitors and 300+ speakers. The festival included the RWA Demo Day, Crypto 2026, Cypher Asia, and HashKey On-Chain Summit. The event reinforced Hong Kong's position as a leading crypto-friendly jurisdiction in Asia, with significant focus on real-world asset tokenization and institutional DeFi.

### Market Trends
Bitcoin continues to dominate the market while most altcoins struggle to keep pace. Despite significant capital in the market, investors remain cautious about riskier assets. The Web3 market has crossed the $68 billion mark in early 2026, with analysts projecting it could surpass $400 billion by decade's end. Q1 2026 also saw several Web3 projects shut down, reflecting ongoing market rationalization.

### Blockchain Identity for AI Agents
Zetrix AI and China's CAICT unveiled Avatar, a blockchain platform providing verified identities and digital asset access for AI agents. As autonomous agents increasingly operate across digital infrastructure, on-chain identity and authentication are becoming critical infrastructure rather than nice-to-have features.
