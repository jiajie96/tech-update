## Detailed Updates

### $292 Million DeFi Exploit and Coordinated Response
On April 18, a $292 million exploit targeting KelpDAO sent shockwaves through DeFi. Stolen tokens were used as collateral to borrow other assets on AAVE, the largest decentralized lending platform, creating cascading risk. AAVE and a coalition of DeFi firms moved quickly, committing over $300 million to stabilize the system. Standard Chartered published analysis noting that despite the shock, DeFi showed improved resilience compared to previous major exploits — a sign of maturing risk management infrastructure. (Source: CoinDesk, April 29)

### SEC Reg Crypto Framework Takes Shape
On April 13, the SEC Division of Trading and Markets issued a five-year DeFi front-end safe harbour — one component of the broader Reg Crypto framework. The tiered approach includes three distinct regulatory instruments: a startup exemption for early-stage projects, an innovation exemption allowing exchanges, DeFi protocols, stablecoin issuers, and DAOs to operate under conditional exemption from full SEC registration, and the front-end safe harbour. This represents the most significant U.S. regulatory clarity for DeFi to date.

### Bipartisan Developer Protection Bill Introduced
Representatives introduced the Promoting Innovation in Blockchain Development Act of 2026 (PIBDA) on February 26, aimed at protecting software developers from misclassification under criminal code Section 1960. The bill targets developers who write code but do not control user funds, addressing a key legal risk that has chilled open-source development in the crypto space.

### Tokenization and TradFi Convergence Accelerates
The blockchain sector is entering a more mature phase, with leading projects solving real financial pain points: cross-border remittances, regulated payment flows, treasury orchestration, and tokenized market access. Ondo Finance has emerged as a key player serving as an issuance and redemption layer for tokenized U.S. stocks and ETFs, exemplifying how crypto infrastructure and traditional securities exposure are converging.
