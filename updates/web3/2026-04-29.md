# Web3 Weekly Update — April 29, 2026

## Highlights

- **Kelp DAO Hacked for $292M**: The largest DeFi exploit of 2026 drained wrapped ether from Kelp DAO, following the $285M Drift exploit earlier in April — raising serious questions about DeFi protocol security.
- **Solana's Alpenglow Consensus Overhaul Progresses**: The full rewrite of Solana's consensus layer, which passed a 98% approval vote, targets 100-150ms finality with new Votor and Rotor components.
- **Israel Approves First Regulated Stablecoin**: BILS, built on Solana with Fireblocks custody and EY auditing, becomes the first government-approved stablecoin in Israel.

## Detailed Updates

### Record DeFi Exploits in April
The DeFi sector faced back-to-back major security incidents in April. The Kelp DAO hack set a new record as the largest DeFi exploit of 2026, draining $292 million in wrapped ether. This followed the $285 million Drift exploit on April 1, where an attacker used a novel durable nonce attack to rapidly take over administrative controls of the Solana-based perpetual futures exchange. The combined $577M in losses in a single month is reigniting debate about the security maturity of DeFi protocols.

### Solana Alpenglow Consensus Upgrade
Solana's Alpenglow upgrade — a full rewrite of its consensus and block propagation layers — continues progressing toward its early-to-mid 2026 target. The upgrade replaces Proof of History and Tower BFT with two new components: Votor for consensus voting and Rotor for block propagation. The goal is to reduce finality to 100-150 milliseconds, a game-changer for trading, gaming, payments, and cross-chain systems where current optimistic confirmations carry meaningful risk. The upgrade passed a governance vote with 98% approval in September 2025.

### Solana P-Token Standard (SIMD-0266)
Solana's SIMD-0266 proposal introduces the P-token standard, designed to replace the existing SPL token program. The new standard targets a 98% reduction in resource usage, which would significantly lower transaction costs for token operations on the network. The launch is scheduled for late 2026.

### Ethereum Glamsterdam Upgrade
Ethereum's 2026 roadmap centers on two upgrades: Glamsterdam (first half of 2026) and Hegota (second half). Glamsterdam focuses on execution efficiency and protocol-level proposer-builder separation, while Hegota addresses state growth, node sustainability, and censorship resistance. Both upgrades represent Ethereum's continued push toward scalability without compromising decentralization.

### Israel's BILS Stablecoin Approval
Israel approved BILS as its first regulated stablecoin, developed in collaboration with the Solana network and crypto custodian Fireblocks, with auditing by EY. This marks a notable milestone in stablecoin regulation, demonstrating that governments are moving toward frameworks that accommodate digital currencies rather than resist them.

### Hong Kong Web3 Festival
Hong Kong hosted a concentration of Web3 events in April including the Web3 Festival, RWA Demo Day, Crypto 2026, and the HashKey On-Chain Summit. Discussions centered on RWA tokenization, AI-crypto convergence, DeFi evolution, and stablecoin frameworks, reinforcing Hong Kong's position as a Web3 hub in Asia.

### Market Conditions
Bitcoin continued to face resistance at the $80,000 level, with derivatives and macro signals pointing to reduced risk appetite and subdued volatility. Most altcoins struggled to keep pace with Bitcoin, and while significant capital remains in the market, investors show hesitancy toward riskier digital assets.

### Web3 Market Milestone
The overall Web3 market size crossed the $68 billion mark in early 2026. Despite setbacks — several Web3 projects shut down in Q1 2026 — blockchain technology is increasingly viewed as a financial and technological operating layer rather than a speculative bet.
