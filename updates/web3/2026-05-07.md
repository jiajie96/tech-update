# Web3 Weekly Update — May 7, 2026

## Highlights

- **Bitcoin Hits Highest Level Since January**: Bitcoin reached $82,305, its highest price since January 31, while Ethereum climbed to $2,412, driven by regulatory optimism and institutional momentum.
- **U.S. Senate Releases CLARITY Act Compromise Text**: The landmark crypto legislation bans stablecoin yield on reserves but allows activity-based rewards, with the White House targeting July 4 for passage.
- **$292M Kelp DAO Exploit Sets 2026 DeFi Hack Record**: The exploit surpasses the $285 million Drift hack from April 1, raising fresh concerns about DeFi protocol security.

## Detailed Updates

### Bitcoin and Ethereum Rally on Regulatory Optimism
Bitcoin reached $82,305.01, its highest price since January 31, while Ethereum touched $2,412.01, the highest since April 27. Over the five-day period, Bitcoin gained 5.4% and Ethereum rose 5.61%. The rally is driven by hopes of renewed geopolitical peace talks, AI sector optimism, and expectations that pending crypto legislation will bring regulatory clarity for U.S. markets.

### CLARITY Act Moves Toward Passage
The U.S. Senate released the CLARITY Act compromise text, which bans stablecoin yield on reserves but allows activity-based "rewards." Polymarket prediction traders assigned more than a 60% probability of passage this year, and the White House has set an ambitious July 4 target date. The legislation represents the most significant potential regulatory framework for crypto in the United States, promising to establish clear rules for stablecoin issuers and digital asset classification.

### Kelp DAO Exploit: $292 Million Lost
A $292 million exploit of Kelp DAO set the new record for the largest DeFi hack in 2026, surpassing the $285 million Drift exploit on April 1. The incident underscores the persistent security challenges facing decentralized finance protocols, even as the industry matures and institutional adoption grows.

### Bitmine Builds Massive Ethereum Treasury
Bitmine, the largest Ethereum treasury firm, purchased 101,745 ETH last week, bringing its total holdings to over 5.18 million ETH. Tom Lee of Fundstrat declared that a "crypto spring" has begun, citing institutional buying activity and improving macro conditions as catalysts for renewed market confidence.

### Wall Street's DTCC Explores Blockchain for Corporate Actions
The Depository Trust & Clearing Corporation (DTCC) is working with layer-1 blockchains to bring millions in corporate actions like dividend payments onchain. The initiative represents one of the most significant moves by traditional financial infrastructure toward blockchain integration, potentially transforming how post-trade processing works for equities and fixed income.

### Consensus Miami 2026 Highlights
At Consensus 2026 in Miami, executives from major Bitcoin lending platforms emphasized that institutional borrowers increasingly prioritize custody, transparency, and standardized lending structures over complex DeFi products — a clear shift from the speculation-driven era. Industry leaders broadly agreed that crypto is evolving beyond speculation into financial infrastructure powering payments, remittances, and broader global access.

### Japan's DeFi Market Surges
Japan completed a revised crypto asset regulatory framework in late 2025 and has emerged as one of the highest-growth DeFi markets globally in 2026. The regulatory clarity has attracted both domestic and international projects, positioning Japan as a leading market for compliant DeFi innovation.

### UX Improvements Drive Adoption
Account abstraction has effectively eliminated seed phrases, making crypto wallets as easy to use as email accounts. Gas sponsorship now allows dApp developers to subsidize transaction fees for users, removing a major friction point in Web3 onboarding. Ethereum's zkEVM and zkSync rollups enable thousands of transactions per second with gas fees dropping to pennies.
