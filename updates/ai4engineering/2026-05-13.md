# AI for Engineering Weekly Update — May 13, 2026

## Highlights

- **Samsung Announces AI-Driven Factory Strategy for 2030**: Samsung Electronics unveiled its plan to transition all global manufacturing operations into AI-driven factories by 2030, integrating AI across the entire manufacturing value chain from logistics to quality inspection.
- **Siemens Digital Twin Composer Coming Mid-2026**: Siemens announced the Digital Twin Composer, combining Siemens digital twin technology with NVIDIA Omniverse simulation, set to be available on the Xcelerator Marketplace in mid-2026.
- **Generalist AI Achieves 99% Robot Task Success Rate**: GEN-1 from Generalist AI pushed average robotic task success rates to 99% on benchmarks where earlier models achieved only 64%, while completing tasks three times faster using just one hour of robot training data.

## Detailed Updates

### Samsung's AI-Driven Factory Vision
Samsung Electronics announced a comprehensive strategy to transition all global manufacturing operations into AI-driven factories by 2030. The plan integrates AI across the entire manufacturing value chain — from inbound material logistics and production processes to quality inspection and final shipment. This represents one of the most ambitious corporate commitments to AI-driven manufacturing from a major electronics manufacturer.

### Siemens Digital Twin Composer with NVIDIA Omniverse
The Digital Twin Composer, set for availability on the Siemens Xcelerator Marketplace in mid-2026, combines Siemens digital twin technology, NVIDIA Omniverse simulation libraries, and real-world engineering data. The tool enables manufacturers to create high-fidelity digital replicas of factory environments, enabling simulation, optimization, and training before physical deployment.

### Generalist AI's GEN-1 Robotics Model
Generalist AI's GEN-1 model achieved a breakthrough in robotic task success, pushing average rates to 99% on tasks where earlier models managed only 64%. The model also completes tasks approximately three times faster and requires only one hour of robot data for training, dramatically lowering the barrier to deploying capable robotic systems in manufacturing and logistics.

### NVIDIA-Led Manufacturing and Robotics Coalition
NVIDIA partnered with major US manufacturing and robotics leaders including Belden, Caterpillar, Foxconn, Lucid Motors, Toyota, TSMC, and Wistron to build Omniverse factory digital twins. The initiative is positioned as driving America's reindustrialization with physical AI, combining simulation, robotics, and digital twin technologies for next-generation manufacturing facilities.

### Terra Innovatum and Sant'Anna University Partner on Autonomous Systems
Terra Innovatum partnered with Sant'Anna University to develop AI solutions for predictive and condition-based maintenance, alongside robotic systems for non-destructive testing and inspection. The collaboration targets performance and lifecycle management of the SOLO micro-modular reactor platform, demonstrating AI's expanding role in nuclear energy infrastructure.

### Digital Twin Market Surging
The global digital twin market is projected to grow from $36.19 billion in 2025 to $180.28 billion by 2030, representing a CAGR of 37.87%. The growth is driven by convergence of AI, IoT, and simulation technologies across manufacturing, energy, and infrastructure sectors.

### AI-IoT-Robotics Convergence in Manufacturing
Industry analysis from May 12 highlights the accelerating convergence of AI, IoT connectivity, and robotics in modern manufacturing. Facilities are increasingly being engineered to identify inefficiencies proactively through predictive analytics, moving from reactive maintenance to AI-driven autonomous optimization of production processes.

### ABB Collaborative Robot Family Expansion
ABB introduced its PoWa collaborative robot family and projected 20% annual cobot market growth through 2028. Rising robot density across Europe, Asia, and the Americas indicates increasing industrial automation adoption, with cobots increasingly handling tasks alongside human workers in mixed environments.
