# Computer Vision Weekly Update — April 29, 2026

## Highlights

- **NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Nano Omni Launches**: Released April 28, this open multimodal model unifies vision, audio, image, and text understanding, topping six leaderboards for document intelligence and video/audio understanding.
- **OpenAI Discontinues Sora, Launches Images 2.0**: OpenAI shut down its Sora video generation tool on April 26 while simultaneously releasing Images 2.0 with dramatically improved text rendering and non-Latin alphabet support.
- **Google Veo 3.1 Lite for High-Volume Video**: Google introduced a lower-cost video generation model supporting text-to-video and image-to-video at up to 1080p, designed for commercial-scale applications.

## Detailed Updates

### NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Nano Omni
NVIDIA released Nemotron 3 Nano Omni on April 28, an open multimodal model that unifies video, audio, image, and text capabilities in a single system. The model is designed for AI agents that need to reason across modalities simultaneously, achieving up to 9x more efficiency than running separate specialized models. It topped six leaderboards for complex document intelligence and media understanding, and is available via Hugging Face, OpenRouter, build.nvidia.com, and 25+ partner platforms.

### OpenAI Sora Discontinued, Images 2.0 Shipped
OpenAI discontinued Sora, its video generation tool, on April 26 — a surprising move that signals a strategic pivot. Meanwhile, OpenAI's new Images 2.0 model represents a major leap in AI image generation, producing accurate text rendering, detailed visuals, and usable assets like menus and marketing materials. The model handles non-Latin alphabets significantly better than predecessors, addressing a long-standing limitation.

### Google Veo 3.1 Lite
Google introduced Veo 3.1 Lite, a cost-optimized video generation model designed for high-volume commercial applications. The model supports both text-to-video and image-to-video generation at up to 1080p resolution, targeting production environments where cost per generation matters more than maximum quality.

### ByteDance Seedance 2.0
ByteDance launched Dreamina Seedance 2.0, an AI video generation model integrated directly into CapCut. The model enables users to create and edit video content using text prompts, reference images, or existing clips. The CapCut integration means the technology reaches CapCut's massive user base immediately rather than requiring a separate tool.

### Image Generators as Vision Learners
Google DeepMind published research showing that image and video generators exhibit zero-shot visual understanding behaviors — similar to how LLMs develop emergent capabilities during pretraining. The finding suggests that image generation training serves as an effective form of visual pretraining, enabling models to learn powerful and general visual representations without explicit supervision.

### 3D Vision Foundation Models Advance
Several 3D vision models gained attention: LingBot-Map, a feed-forward 3D foundation model that reconstructs scenes from video streams at 20 FPS using a geometric context transformer; and HY-World 2.0, a multi-modal world model framework generating high-fidelity 3D Gaussian Splatting scenes from diverse inputs. Reallusion also announced its 2026 vision for redefining 3D production through hybrid AI approaches.

### Edge AI Vision Awards
Nota AI's Vision Agent won the 2026 Edge AI and Vision Product of the Year Award in the Edge AI Large Multimodal Models category, highlighting the push toward efficient on-device vision AI.

### CVPR 2026 Preview
CVPR 2026, scheduled for June 3-7 at the Colorado Convention Center in Denver, has released paper previews indicating strong representation of topics in embodied vision, 3D reconstruction, and vision-language models.
