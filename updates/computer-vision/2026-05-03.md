# Computer Vision Weekly Update — May 3, 2026

## Highlights

- **Midjourney launches V1 video model**: The image generation leader enters video with 5-second motion clips at 25x lower cost than competitors, challenging Sora and Veo.
- **OpenAI discontinues original Sora**: Sora was officially shut down April 26, while Sora2 continues with improved temporal consistency and physics simulation.
- **Waymo and Tesla advance vision-based 3D reconstruction**: New approaches eliminate LiDAR dependence through camera-only perception systems and world models for autonomous driving simulation.

## Detailed Updates

### Midjourney Enters Video Generation with V1
Midjourney launched its first video model, V1, which animates images into five-second motion clips. Pricing is notably competitive — one video job costs about the same as an image upscale, making it roughly 25 times cheaper than most AI video services. While Midjourney isn't competing head-to-head with Google Veo or OpenAI's Sora2 on cinematic 4K output, its cost advantage and integration with its existing image platform could drive widespread adoption among creative professionals.

### OpenAI Shuts Down Original Sora, Sora2 Carries On
OpenAI officially discontinued its original video generation tool Sora on April 26, 2026. Sora2, the second-generation model released in late 2025, continues with significant improvements in temporal consistency, physics simulation, and integration with Microsoft Azure cloud environments. The discontinuation highlights the rapid iteration cycles in generative video AI.

### Google DeepMind's Agentic Vision in Gemini 3 Flash
Google DeepMind introduced Agentic Vision to Gemini 3 Flash, combining visual reasoning with Python code execution to improve image analysis. The system enables active visual investigations rather than passive image description — the model can write and run code to analyze visual data, measure properties, and draw conclusions.

### Tesla FSD: Complete 3D World Reconstruction from Cameras
Tesla's Full Self-Driving vision system continues to push the boundaries of camera-only perception, reconstructing complete 3D environments from 2D camera feeds through a five-step transformation process. Multiple camera angles are fused through transformer neural networks to create a seamless 3D vector space, with temporal alignment merging consecutive frames to capture both static and dynamic elements.

### Wayve's Rig3R: Multi-Camera 3D Reconstruction in One Pass
Wayve presented Rig3R at NeurIPS, introducing a rig encoder that injects known camera rig constraints into the transformer's decoder. The method performs dense 3D reconstruction, camera pose estimation, and rig calibration in a single forward pass — advancing efficient multi-camera perception for autonomous vehicles.

### Waymo World Model Powered by DeepMind's Genie 3
The Waymo World Model, built on Google DeepMind's Genie 3 general-purpose world model, generates high-fidelity multi-sensor outputs including both camera and lidar data. It can simulate extremely rare events — from tornadoes to animal encounters — that are nearly impossible to capture in real-world driving data, addressing a critical bottleneck in autonomous vehicle validation.

### Computer Vision Conference 2026 Set for Amsterdam
The Computer Vision Conference (CVC) 2026 is scheduled for May 21–22 in Amsterdam, bringing together researchers and practitioners to discuss advances in vision AI, image processing, and deep learning applications.

### Synthetic Data Gains Momentum for Vision AI Training
Gartner identifies synthetic data as a critical alternative for Vision AI projects in 2026, keeping projects compliant while enabling augmented simulations and faster R&D. The trend reflects growing challenges with acquiring and labeling real-world training data at scale.
