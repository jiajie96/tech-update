## Detailed Updates

### Vision Banana — Google DeepMind (April 22)

Google DeepMind published Vision Banana, an instruction-tuned image generator built by mixing computer vision task data into a state-of-the-art base model. The results are striking: Vision Banana outperforms SAM 3 on segmentation benchmarks and surpasses Depth Anything V3 on metric depth estimation. Rather than training a specialist model for each task, the approach folds multiple CV objectives into a single generative backbone. This continues the trend of foundation models absorbing capabilities that previously required dedicated architectures, and it raises the question of how long standalone segmentation and depth pipelines will remain competitive.

### AI Video Generation — The Landscape Shifts

The video generation space has moved fast over the past year. In early 2024, state-of-the-art clips topped out at 4-10 seconds with noticeable artifacts. Today, leading models produce coherent 2-minute single-pass generations with consistent subjects, lighting, and motion.

Current positioning across major players:

- **Veo 3.1** (Google) is the safest overall pick — strong realism, reliable motion, and native audio generation make it a solid default for most workflows.
- **Kling 3.0** offers the strongest value proposition, delivering competitive quality at lower cost.
- **Seedance 2.0** has emerged as the hottest option for image-to-video tasks specifically.
- **Runway Gen-4.5** remains the go-to choice when fine-grained control over generation parameters matters most.
- **HappyHorse-1.0** from Alibaba climbed to the top of blind-test rankings for both text-to-video and image-to-video, making it a serious contender from the Chinese ecosystem.

Meanwhile, OpenAI's **Sora app shut down on April 26**, with full API discontinuation planned for September 24. The closure marks a notable exit from a product that generated enormous hype at launch but struggled to find sustainable footing against rapidly improving competitors.

### Energy Efficiency Breakthrough

Researchers unveiled a new approach that slashes AI energy use by approximately 100x while simultaneously improving accuracy. The method combines neural networks with symbolic reasoning, targeting robotic applications where inference must be both fast and power-efficient. If the technique generalizes beyond its initial demonstrations, it could meaningfully change the cost calculus for deploying vision models at the edge — a persistent bottleneck for factory automation, autonomous vehicles, and mobile robotics.

### Synthetic Data Momentum

Gartner projects that by 2028, 70% of computer vision models will depend on multimodal training data that includes synthetic sources. The driver is straightforward: real-world data collection remains expensive, slow, and constrained by privacy regulations and long-tail distribution gaps. Expect continued investment in simulation engines and generative pipelines designed to produce photorealistic, physically plausible training samples at scale.

### Upcoming — CVPR 2026 (June 3-7, Denver)

CVPR 2026 is shaping up to be a landmark event. Of particular note is the **ManipArena Competition**, a real-robot manipulation challenge that evaluates physical reasoning across 20 distinct tasks. This is a meaningful step beyond simulation-only benchmarks and will provide a clearer picture of where embodied AI actually stands.

### Hannover Messe — Industrial Vision in Practice

At this year's Hannover Messe, several exhibitors demonstrated vision AI agents and humanoid robots operating in factory settings. The emphasis was on practical deployment rather than research demos — systems performing inspection, assembly assistance, and material handling alongside human workers. Industrial adoption of vision AI continues its steady acceleration.
