## Detailed Updates

### Vision Banana: Image Generators as Generalist Vision Models (April 25)

Google DeepMind published "Image Generators are Generalist Vision Learners" on April 25, introducing Vision Banana — a model built by instruction-tuning the Nano Banana Pro image generator on a small amount of vision task data. The key finding is that image generators develop zero-shot visual understanding capabilities analogous to how LLMs develop emergent abilities from generative pretraining. Vision Banana achieves 0.699 mIoU on Cityscapes (4.7 points above SAM 3), surpasses Depth Anything V3 on metric depth estimation (0.929 vs. 0.918 δ1), and beats Lotus-2 on surface normal estimation — all in zero-shot transfer. By parameterizing vision task outputs as RGB images, a single generative model replaces separate specialist models for segmentation, depth, surface normals, and image editing. The research suggests a paradigm shift where generative pretraining becomes central to building foundational vision models.

### ChatGPT Images 2.0 (April 21)

OpenAI's ChatGPT Images 2.0 advances the state of the art in image generation with native reasoning capabilities — the model "thinks" before generating. It achieves ~99% text accuracy across scripts including Japanese, Arabic, Korean, and Bengali, supports 2K resolution with flexible aspect ratios, and can render 100+ objects in a single coherent scene. The model scored 1,512 on the Image Arena leaderboard, leading second place by 242 points. DALL-E 2 and DALL-E 3 will be retired on May 12.

### CVPR 2026 Paper Highlights (Announced This Week)

CVPR 2026 (June 3-7, Denver) accepted over 4,000 papers, with highlights announced this week including:

- **Holo360D**: A large-scale real-world dataset with continuous trajectories for advancing panoramic 3D reconstruction (oral presentation).
- **ManipArena Competition**: The Embodied AI Workshop will host a real-robot manipulation challenge evaluating models across 20 real-world tasks.
- **Knowledge Visualization**: A benchmark for knowledge-intensive text-to-image generation.

Major conference themes include generative models for image synthesis, efficient vision transformers, self-supervised learning, and robust perception for autonomous systems.
