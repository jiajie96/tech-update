# Computer Vision Weekly Update — April 28, 2026

## Highlights

- **Google DeepMind's Vision Banana Unifies Generation and Understanding**: An instruction-tuned image generator that beats SAM 3 on segmentation and Depth Anything V3 on depth estimation, signaling a paradigm shift where generative models become general-purpose vision systems.
- **Tesla FSD Reconstructs Full 3D Worlds from Camera Feeds**: Vision-only approach eliminates LiDAR dependency while achieving comprehensive scene understanding.
- **3D Gaussian Splatting Emerges as Dominant Reconstruction Method**: Rapid adoption across autonomous driving, robotics, and content creation applications.

## Detailed Updates

### Vision Banana: Generative Models as Vision Foundation Models

Google DeepMind introduced Vision Banana, built upon Nano Banana Pro by instruction-tuning on a mixture of original training data alongside vision task data. The key insight is that image generators exhibit zero-shot visual understanding behaviors — similar to how large language models develop emergent capabilities from generative pretraining. Vision Banana surpasses SAM 3 on three segmentation benchmarks, Depth Anything V3 on metric depth estimation, and Lotus-2 on surface normal estimation, all in zero-shot transfer settings. Instead of requiring separate models for each vision task, a single image generation model serves as the common interface for semantic segmentation, instance segmentation, depth estimation, surface normal estimation, image generation, and image editing. This research suggests a major paradigm shift where generative vision pretraining takes a central role in building foundational vision models.

### Tesla FSD Vision-Only 3D Reconstruction

Tesla's Full Self-Driving system continues to advance its vision-only approach to autonomous driving, reconstructing complete three-dimensional worlds from 2D camera feeds using advanced neural networks. The system's surface understanding discerns road types, elevations, and material compositions, distinguishing features such as curbs, lane markings, and obstacles purely through visual data interpretation. This represents a key departure from traditional systems that rely on LiDAR sensors, demonstrating that camera-based perception can achieve comprehensive scene understanding at lower hardware costs.

### 3D Gaussian Splatting Takes Over Scene Reconstruction

3D Gaussian Splatting (3D GS) has become the dominant research direction in 3D scene reconstruction, with particularly vigorous development in autonomous driving applications. The technique offers real-time rendering of photorealistic scenes while maintaining compact representations, making it practical for deployment in resource-constrained environments. Researchers are now applying 3D GS to everything from urban scene mapping to indoor navigation and robotic manipulation.

### DriveTok: Unified 3D Scene Tokenization

DriveTok, a new 3D driving scene tokenizer, enables unified multi-view reconstruction and understanding. The system extracts semantically rich visual features from vision foundation models and transforms them into scene tokens using 3D deformable cross-attention. This unified tokenization approach allows a single architecture to handle both scene reconstruction and semantic understanding, improving efficiency for autonomous driving perception stacks.

### Vision-Based 3D Occupancy Prediction

Vision-based 3D occupancy prediction — predicting spatial occupancy status and semantics of 3D voxel grids from camera images — has matured into a practical perception capability for cost-effective autonomous driving systems. The approach provides dense 3D understanding of driving scenes without requiring expensive depth sensors, making it particularly attractive for scaling autonomous driving to mass-market vehicles.

### CVPR 2026 Preview

CVPR 2026, scheduled for June 3-7 in Denver, Colorado, will feature major themes including advances in generative models for image synthesis, efficient vision transformers, self-supervised learning, and robust perception for autonomous systems. The Workshop on Autonomous Driving is expected to be one of the most attended events, reflecting the field's growing emphasis on real-world deployment of vision systems.
