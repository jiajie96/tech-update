# Computer Vision Weekly Update — May 5, 2026

## Highlights

- **Google Veo 3.1 dominates AI video generation with 96.4% market share**: The video generation market has consolidated dramatically, with OpenAI's Sora 2 capturing just 2.0% as monthly order volumes surged 5x since December 2025.
- **Novi AI's Long Video Agent enables 5-minute coherent videos**: A new "director" architecture maintains global scene memory, achieving consistent lighting, weather, and backgrounds across long-form generated content.
- **Multimodal vision models become the dominant paradigm**: Vision systems now combine images with text, audio, and sensor data, with models like InternVL3-78B and Meta's SAM 3 leading the field.

## Detailed Updates

### Google Veo 3.1 Achieves Near-Total Market Dominance

The AI video generation market has undergone dramatic consolidation. Google's Veo 3.1 commands 96.4% market share, with OpenAI's Sora 2 at just 2.0%. Monthly order volumes exploded from 12,000 in December 2025 to 62,000 in January 2026 — a 5x increase in a single month. According to Statista's 2026 Digital Marketing Report, 67% of brands now use AI-generated video for at least some social media content.

### Novi AI Long Video Agent: A Paradigm Shift

Novi AI launched the Long Video Agent in April 2026, representing a fundamental shift in video generation. Instead of frame-by-frame generation, the Long Video Agent acts as a "director" maintaining global memory of the scene. This enables videos up to 5 minutes long with perfectly consistent lighting, weather, and background details. The system uses convolutional neural networks to verify spatial consistency with 99% pixel accuracy.

### Scene Stability as the New Benchmark

In 2026, high scene stability has become the primary benchmark for professional-grade AI video production. The focus is on ensuring characters, backgrounds, and lighting remain uniform from first frame to last. This shift reflects the industry moving from "can we generate video?" to "can we generate video that's production-ready?"

### Multimodal Vision Models Lead the Field

The dominant paradigm for 2026 AI vision systems is multimodality — processing and generating synchronized data from diverse sources. InternVL3-78B leads as an open-source industrial-level vision-language model with 3D reasoning capabilities. Meta's SAM 3 dominates segmentation, accepting multimodal prompts (text, bounding boxes, points, masks) to generate precise masks.

### 3D Vision: LingBot-Map and SpatialLM

LingBot-Map is a feed-forward 3D foundation model that reconstructs scenes from video streams at 20 FPS using a geometric context transformer architecture. SpatialLM processes 3D point cloud data from monocular video, RGBD images, and LiDAR sensors to generate structured 3D scene understandings. These advances enable applications from autonomous drones to real-time surgical mapping.

### NVIDIA RTX Accelerates 4K AI Video on PC

NVIDIA announced RTX acceleration for 4K AI video generation on PC with LTX-2 and ComfyUI upgrades, bringing high-quality video generation capabilities to consumer hardware. This democratizes access to AI video tools that previously required cloud compute.

### Computer Vision Trends: Autonomy and Synthetic Data

SKY ENGINE AI reports that real-world data will increasingly fall short for computer vision projects in 2026, driving demand for synthetic data generation. The industry is trending toward autonomy-focused applications, digital twin integration, and hybrid training approaches that combine real and synthetic datasets.
