# Computer Vision Weekly Update — May 7, 2026

## Highlights

- **DeepMind Releases Vision Banana**: A single-weight generalist model that beats specialists on segmentation, depth estimation, and surface normals through prompt-based task switching — led by Kaiming He and Saining Xie.
- **Seedance 2.0 Tops Video Generation Rankings**: Claiming the overall top spot for AI video generation in 2026 with unmatched motion quality, prompt adherence, and pricing at $0.022/sec for production-grade output.
- **SpatialLM Pushes 3D Scene Understanding**: A new 3D large language model excels at processing point cloud data from monocular video, RGBD, and LiDAR for structured spatial reasoning.

## Detailed Updates

### DeepMind's Vision Banana: One Model to Rule Them All
DeepMind released Vision Banana, an image foundation model that achieves specialist-level performance across segmentation, depth estimation, and surface normal prediction through a single set of weights. The model switches between tasks by prompt alone, eliminating the need for separate specialized models. Led by notable researchers Kaiming He and Saining Xie, this represents a significant step toward generalist visual AI that collapses the traditional vendor moat around task-specific vision models.

### Seedance 2.0 Leads Video Generation
Seedance 2.0 has taken the top spot for overall best AI video generation model in 2026, with its combination of motion quality, prompt adherence, and price performance unmatched at $0.022/sec for production-grade output. The video generation landscape has matured considerably, with Alibaba's latest model earning praise for photorealism and physics consistency across long durations. Google's Veo 3.1, released in January 2026, brought native 4K resolution, vertical video support, and improved character consistency.

### SpatialLM Advances 3D Understanding
SpatialLM, a recent 3D large language model, excels at processing 3D point cloud data from diverse sources including monocular video sequences, RGBD images, and LiDAR sensors. The model generates structured 3D scene understandings, with robust spatial reasoning capabilities positioning it as an essential tool for autonomous navigation, embodied robotics, and detailed 3D scene analysis.

### LingBot-Map: Real-Time 3D Reconstruction
LingBot-Map is a feed-forward 3D foundation model that reconstructs scenes from video streams using a geometric context transformer architecture with specialized attention mechanisms. The model handles coordinate grounding, dense geometric cues, and long-range drift correction, achieving stable real-time performance at 20 FPS — a critical threshold for practical robotics and AR applications.

### Multimodal Vision Models Reach New Heights
The latest multimodal vision models are closing the gap with proprietary frontier systems. Qwen3-VL-235B rivals top-tier models like Gemini-2.5-Pro and GPT-5 across benchmarks covering general Q&A, 2D/3D grounding, video understanding, OCR, and document comprehension. GLM-4.5V offers state-of-the-art capabilities with efficient MoE architecture and advanced 3D spatial reasoning using 3D-RoPE encoding. InternVL3-78B provides industrial-grade open-source vision capabilities.

### Google I/O 2026 Expected to Reveal Omni Model
Industry reports indicate a full reveal and potential public beta of Google's Omni model during Google I/O 2026. The model is expected to unify text, image, video, and audio understanding in a single architecture, continuing the industry shift toward omni-modal systems that process all sensory inputs natively.

### Video AI Market Matures
According to Statista's April 2026 report, global demand for text-to-video generation has surged as businesses look to automate personalized advertising at scale. The competitive landscape has shifted from model size to operational capabilities: training from real and synthetic experience, measuring behavior over time, and deploying reliably under constraints like latency, compression, privacy, and drift.
