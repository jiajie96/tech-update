# Computer Vision Weekly Update — April 30, 2026

## Highlights

- **OpenAI discontinues Sora**: The video generation tool's web and app experiences were shut down April 26, with the API remaining available until September 2026 — a notable retreat from consumer video generation.
- **NVIDIA launches Nemotron 3 Nano Omni**: A 30B-parameter multimodal model unifying vision, audio, image, and text for edge AI agents, featuring a hybrid MoE architecture with 256K context.
- **Seedance 2.0 emerges as top video generation model**: The model is dominating blind creator tests, particularly in image-to-video workflows, while Google's Veo 3.1 remains the overall leader.

## Detailed Updates

### OpenAI Discontinues Sora
OpenAI announced on April 26 that Sora's web and mobile app experiences have been discontinued. The API will remain available until September 24, 2026. The shutdown marks a significant shift — OpenAI appears to be retreating from consumer-facing video generation to focus on API-level integration. The move comes as competitors like Google (Veo 3.1) and ByteDance (Seedance 2.0) have overtaken Sora in quality benchmarks and creator adoption.

### NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Nano Omni
Released April 28, NVIDIA's Nemotron 3 Nano Omni is an open multimodal model that brings vision, audio, image, and text capabilities into one system. Built on a 30B-A3B hybrid MoE architecture with Conv3D and 256K context, it's designed for edge AI agents that need to process multiple modalities simultaneously. The model enables agents to deliver faster responses with advanced reasoning across video, audio, image, and text inputs.

### Video Generation Landscape Shifts
The video generation market has matured significantly, with tools moving from experimental to enterprise-grade. Veo 3.1 remains the top overall pick for combining realism, motion quality, and native audio. Seedance 2.0 is the breakout model of the moment, consistently winning blind creator tests and excelling in image-to-video workflows. The industry is seeing the first stages of "4D Video Generation," where AI creates volumetric video explorable in VR and AR environments.

### Google DeepMind: Image Generators as Vision Learners
Google DeepMind published research showing that image generation models can serve as generalist vision learners, performing tasks like depth estimation, segmentation, and surface normal prediction without task-specific training. The work suggests that the representations learned during image generation are more generally useful than previously understood, potentially unifying generative and discriminative vision approaches.

### 3D Vision and Spatial Understanding Advances
LingBot-Map, a feed-forward 3D foundation model, achieves stable real-time 3D reconstruction from video streams at 20 FPS using a geometric context transformer architecture. Meanwhile, HY-World 2.0 generates high-fidelity 3D Gaussian Splatting scenes from diverse inputs, pushing the boundary of scene generation from 2D to 3D. These advances are driving progress in robotics, AR, and autonomous navigation.

### Gemma 4 for Computer Vision
Google's Gemma 4 release includes models with strong vision capabilities. The open-source family offers computer vision engineers production-ready models for tasks like object detection, image classification, and visual question answering, with the 31B Dense variant performing competitively against much larger proprietary models.

### Edge AI and Vision Convergence
The edge AI and vision community reports continued convergence of on-device inference and visual understanding. Advances in model compression and specialized hardware are enabling real-time computer vision on mobile and embedded devices, expanding applications in retail analytics, industrial inspection, and autonomous systems.
