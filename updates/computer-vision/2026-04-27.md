# Computer Vision Weekly Update — April 27, 2026

## Highlights

- **Google DeepMind launches Vision Banana**: A unified model that beats SAM 3 on segmentation and Depth Anything V3 on metric depth — while retaining image generation capabilities.
- **Alibaba's HappyHorse-1.0 tops video benchmarks**: A mysterious AI video model confirmed as Alibaba's project climbed to #1 on blind-test rankings for both text-to-video and image-to-video.
- **Kling 3.0 delivers broadcast-quality 4K at 60fps**: First AI video model to meet broadcast delivery standards without upscaling.

## Detailed Updates

### Google DeepMind Vision Banana
Published April 22, Vision Banana is a unified, instruction-tuned image model that surpasses state-of-the-art specialist systems across semantic segmentation, instance segmentation, monocular metric depth estimation, and surface normal estimation — all while retaining image generation capabilities. This "one model to rule them all" approach challenges the assumption that vision tasks require specialized architectures.

### Alibaba HappyHorse-1.0 — Surprise Video Generation Leader
Around April 7, a mysterious model appeared on the Artificial Analysis benchmarking platform and quickly climbed to the top of blind-test rankings. On April 10, CNBC confirmed it as Alibaba's HappyHorse-1.0, which leads both text-to-video and image-to-video generation categories. The stealth launch and rapid ascent demonstrate the intense competition in video generation.

### Kling 3.0 — Broadcast-Ready AI Video
Kling 3.0 became the first AI video model to meet broadcast delivery standards without upscaling, offering native 4K at 60fps. Its storyboard feature generates up to six camera cuts in a single generation with visual consistency, making it viable for professional content production.

### AI Video Generation State of the Art
The gap between early 2025 and 2026 is dramatic: clip length has gone from 4-10 seconds in 2024 to coherent two-minute single-pass generations, with segment chaining enabling 5+ minute videos. Google's Veo 3.1 is regarded as the safest overall pick, combining strong realism, motion, and native audio.

### NVIDIA RTX-Powered 4K Video Generation
NVIDIA introduced an RTX-powered video pipeline enabling 3x faster generations and upscaling to 4K using LTX-2. The open-source solution delivers results competing with leading cloud-based models while generating up to 20 seconds of 4K video locally on consumer hardware.

### CVPR 2026 Preview
The 2026 Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (June 3-7, Denver) will showcase advances in embodied AI, robotics, multi-modal AI, wearable AI, spatial computing, and AI agents. The ManipArena Competition will evaluate real-robot manipulation models across 20 real-world tasks.

### Figure 03 Autonomous Cleanup
Figure's humanoid robot demonstrated autonomous living room cleanup powered by the Helix 02 VLA (Vision-Language-Action) model, showcasing the convergence of computer vision with physical robotics in domestic settings.

### Synthetic Data Becomes Essential
Gartner projects that by 2028, 70% of computer vision models will depend on multimodal training data — a level of coverage that cannot be achieved through real-world collection alone. This validates the growing investment in synthetic data generation pipelines.
