# Computer Vision Weekly Update — April 27, 2026

## Highlights

- **Google DeepMind launches Vision Banana**: A unified model that beats SAM 3 on segmentation and Depth Anything V3 on metric depth — proving image generators are generalist vision learners.
- **Alibaba's HappyHorse-1.0 tops video generation benchmarks**: Stealth-launched model climbed to #1 on blind-test rankings for both text-to-video and image-to-video.
- **67% of brands now use AI-generated video for social content**: Video-first AI has moved from experimental to mainstream production infrastructure.

## Detailed Updates

### Google DeepMind Vision Banana
Published April 22, the paper "Image Generators are Generalist Vision Learners" introduces Vision Banana, built by instruction-tuning Nano Banana Pro on a mixture of original training data and vision task data. The single unified model surpasses state-of-the-art specialists across semantic segmentation, instance segmentation, monocular metric depth estimation, and surface normal estimation — while retaining image generation capabilities. This challenges the assumption that vision tasks require specialized architectures.

### Alibaba HappyHorse-1.0 — Surprise Video Leader
Around April 7, a mysterious model appeared on Artificial Analysis benchmarks and rapidly took the #1 spot. CNBC confirmed it on April 10 as Alibaba's HappyHorse-1.0, which leads both text-to-video and image-to-video generation categories. The stealth launch demonstrates the intensity of competition in generative video.

### Kling 3.0 — First Broadcast-Ready AI Video
Kling 3.0 became the first AI video model to meet broadcast delivery standards without upscaling, offering native 4K at 60fps. Its storyboard feature generates up to six camera cuts in a single generation with visual consistency, making it viable for professional broadcast production.

### Wan2.2 — Leading Open-Source Video Model
Wan2.2, the current leading open-source video generation model, introduced a Mixture-of-Experts architecture into video diffusion models. Trained on 1.5 billion videos and 10 billion images, it offers an accessible alternative to proprietary solutions and has released both inference code and model weights.

### AI Video Generation Reaches Maturity
The state of the art has shifted dramatically: clip length went from 4-10 seconds in 2024 to coherent two-minute single-pass generations, with segment chaining enabling 5+ minute videos. Runway Gen-3, Pika, and Google Veo 3.1 have democratized video production. Statista's 2026 Digital Marketing Report shows 67% of brands now use AI-generated video for social media content.

### NVIDIA RTX Local Video Generation
NVIDIA's RTX-powered pipeline enables 3x faster video generation and 4K upscaling using LTX-2 on consumer hardware. Generating up to 20 seconds of 4K video locally, it competes with cloud-based solutions while keeping data and compute on-device.

### CVPR 2026 Preview
The Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (June 3-7, Denver) will feature advances in embodied AI, robotics, multi-modal AI, wearable AI, spatial computing, and AI agents. The ManipArena Competition will evaluate real-robot manipulation across 20 tasks.

### Multimodal & Video-First Paradigm
The dominant paradigm shift in 2026 is multimodality — AI systems processing and generating synchronized data across modalities. Advances in hardware and declining compute costs are turning video-first AI from experimental to practical across industries from manufacturing quality inspection to autonomous driving.
