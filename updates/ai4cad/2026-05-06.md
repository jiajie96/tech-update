# AI for CAD Weekly Update — May 6, 2026

## Highlights

- **Dassault Systèmes ships SOLIDWORKS 2026 with embedded generative AI**: AI is now integrated directly into drawing creation, detailing, and assembly modeling workflows, with new Virtual Companions LEO and MARIE for interactive collaboration.
- **Autodesk advances Neural CAD foundation model**: A text-to-CAD AI model capable of generating fully editable geometry from natural language prompts represents Autodesk's most disruptive development in years.
- **Siemens Solid Edge adds AI-automated drawings**: The latest version generates up to 80% of drawing views with minimal user input, drastically reducing documentation time.

## Detailed Updates

### SOLIDWORKS 2026 Embeds Generative AI

Dassault Systèmes' release of SOLIDWORKS 2026 marks a significant milestone in normalizing generative AI within everyday mechanical design. The release embeds AI directly into drawing creation, detailing, and assembly modeling. Two new Virtual Companions — LEO and MARIE — are designed for interactive collaboration rather than one-off AI tool calls, suggesting a shift toward persistent AI co-pilots in CAD workflows. These companions can help with design exploration, constraint checking, and documentation.

### Autodesk's Neural CAD Foundation Model

Autodesk's most disruptive development is their work on Neural CAD — an AI foundation model capable of generating fully editable CAD geometry from a single text prompt. While still in development, this represents a fundamental shift from parametric modeling to intent-based design. The promise is that engineers could describe what they need in natural language and receive manufacturable geometry, though the gap between marketing claims and production-ready capabilities remains significant.

### Siemens Solid Edge AI-Automated Drawings

The latest version of Siemens Solid Edge added an AI feature that generates up to 80% of drawing views with minimal user input. This targets one of the most time-consuming aspects of engineering workflows — documentation and drawing creation — and represents the category of AI-CAD applications delivering immediate, measurable ROI.

### Generative Design vs. Design Review: Where ROI Lives

Industry analysis from CoLab Software argues that if near-term ROI is the goal, generative design is not where the clearest value lies today. The more mature, immediately deployable application of AI in engineering workflows is automated design review — catching errors, enforcing standards, and validating manufacturability. Text-to-CAD, while exciting, still has the widest gap between marketing promises and production reality.

### AI-Powered Design Optimization Matures

Engineers can now input constraints — materials, weight, cost, and performance goals — and receive hundreds of optimized design alternatives from generative design systems. However, the key challenge remains translating AI-generated topologies into manufacturable parts with proper tolerances, draft angles, and material specifications that work in real production environments.

### Market Trends

Every major CAD vendor now has AI features either shipping or in beta. The three dominant applications emerging across all platforms are: automated drawing/documentation generation, AI-assisted design review, and generative design exploration. The consensus is that AI augmentation of existing workflows delivers faster payback than fully autonomous design generation.
