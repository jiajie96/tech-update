# AI for CAD Weekly Update — May 4, 2026

## Highlights

- **Autodesk Previews 3D Generative AI Foundation Models**: Autodesk announced upcoming 3D generative AI foundation models designed to transform how designers create and iterate on 3D geometry directly within professional workflows.
- **SOLIDWORKS Introduces LEO and MARIE Virtual Companions**: Two AI companions are coming to SOLIDWORKS Design in 2026 — LEO for mechanical engineering and MARIE for materials science and research collaboration.
- **SketchUp AI Render Goes Live**: Trimble released SketchUp AI Render, making SketchUp the first major CAD program to ship integrated generative rendering capabilities.

## Detailed Updates

### Autodesk 3D Generative AI Foundation Models

Autodesk announced upcoming 3D generative AI foundation models that will integrate directly into its design platforms. These models represent a move beyond simple text-to-3D generation toward professional-grade geometry creation that respects engineering constraints. The models are designed to work within existing Autodesk workflows rather than as standalone tools, addressing the long-standing gap between consumer-grade AI 3D generation and production CAD requirements.

### SOLIDWORKS Virtual Companions: LEO and MARIE

Dassault Systèmes announced two new Virtual Companions for SOLIDWORKS Design, accessible through the 3DEXPERIENCE platform. LEO specializes in mechanical design, assisting with engineering workflows, dimensioning, and design optimization. MARIE brings scientific research skills with expertise in materials science, hypothesis development, microbiology, and chemistry. Both companions are designed for interactive collaboration rather than simple command execution.

### SketchUp Ships First Integrated Generative Rendering

Trimble released SketchUp AI Render, making it the first major CAD program to offer built-in generative rendering. The feature uses AI to quickly create realistic renders of 3D models, with text prompts enabling rapid experimentation across materials, lighting, and environmental variations. This significantly reduces the traditional rendering pipeline from hours to seconds for early-stage design visualization.

### Siemens Solid Edge AI Drawing Automation

The latest version of Siemens Solid Edge added an AI feature that generates up to 80% of drawing views with minimal user input. The system analyzes assemblies and determines appropriate projection views, section cuts, and detail views automatically. Combined with Autodesk Fusion's Automated Drawings feature (which uses AI to classify standard fasteners and exclude them from drawings), automated drawing generation is becoming a standard CAD capability.

### AI-Driven Design Optimization Matures

Engineers can now input constraints such as materials, weight, cost, and performance goals, and AI generates hundreds or thousands of optimized design alternatives. A Gartner report suggests that by 2026, a majority of digital design workflows will include some level of AI-assisted modeling. The shift from static asset generation to interactive, real-time design iteration represents the biggest change in CAD workflows since parametric modeling.

### Leo AI for Engineering CAD

Leo AI continues to develop its platform for generating manufacturing-ready CAD models from natural language descriptions. The platform focuses on producing parametric, editable models rather than mesh-based outputs — addressing the key limitation that has prevented AI-generated 3D content from being useful in professional engineering contexts.

### 3D AI Moves Toward 4D Experiences

The broader 3D AI landscape in 2026 is shifting from static asset generation to interactive, real-time, and increasingly "4D" experiences. Key advances include better text-to-mesh quality, faster workflows, stronger real-time rendering integration, and more agent-like tools that can participate in iterative design processes rather than just producing one-shot outputs.
