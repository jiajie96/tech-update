# AI for CAD Weekly Update — May 13, 2026

## Highlights

- **MIT's PhysiOpt Merges Generative AI with Physics Simulation**: MIT CSAIL developed PhysiOpt, a system that augments generative AI models with physics simulations to ensure 3D blueprints are structurally sound and functional in the real world.
- **Siemens Solid Edge AI Generates 80% of Drawing Views Automatically**: Siemens added an AI feature to Solid Edge that generates up to 80% of drawing views with minimal user input, dramatically accelerating documentation workflows.
- **Text-to-CAD Becomes the Hottest Development Area in 2026**: Natural language-to-3D-model generation is seeing the most active development across the CAD industry, with multiple vendors racing to ship production-ready solutions.

## Detailed Updates

### MIT PhysiOpt: Physics-Aware Generative Design
MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory developed PhysiOpt, a system that pairs generative AI models with physics simulations. The system ensures that AI-generated 3D blueprints are not just visually correct but structurally sound and functionally viable. This addresses one of the biggest criticisms of generative design — that outputs often look plausible but fail under real-world engineering constraints.

### Siemens Solid Edge AI Drawing Automation
Siemens added an AI feature to its Solid Edge CAD platform that can automatically generate up to 80% of the drawing views needed for an assembly with minimal user input. This significantly reduces the time engineers spend on documentation, one of the most tedious aspects of the CAD workflow. The feature combines heuristics with learned patterns from existing drawing sets.

### Text-to-CAD Leads Active Development
Text-to-CAD — where engineers describe parts or assemblies in natural language and AI produces 3D models — has emerged as the most actively developed frontier in CAD AI during 2026. Multiple vendors including Leo AI, Autodesk, and several startups are racing to ship production-ready solutions, though current systems still require significant human refinement for engineering-grade outputs.

### Autodesk Fusion Automated Drawings
Autodesk Fusion's Automated Drawings feature combines templates and heuristics with AI to create technical drawings for assemblies. The system currently uses an AI model to classify standard fasteners and exclude them from drawings automatically, streamlining the documentation process for complex assemblies.

### SOLIDWORKS Virtual Companions: LEO and MARIE
Dassault Systemes announced two new Virtual Companions — LEO and MARIE — coming to SOLIDWORKS Design in 2026. Accessible through the 3DEXPERIENCE platform, these AI assistants are designed for interactive collaboration during the design process, offering contextual guidance and design suggestions.

### SketchUp AI Render Gains Traction
SketchUp's AI Render feature, launched in December 2025, continues to gain adoption as the first generative rendering tool integrated into a major CAD program. The feature uses AI to create realistic renders from 3D models via text prompts, without the traditional setup of lighting and materials, allowing rapid exploration of design variations.

### Generative Physics Models Breakthrough
New research on generative physics models is bridging the gap between AI-generated geometry and engineering simulation. These models can predict physical behavior during the generation process itself, rather than requiring a separate simulation step, potentially collapsing the design-validate cycle from hours to minutes.
