# AI for CAD Weekly Update — April 27, 2026

## Highlights

- **Autodesk Neural CAD aims to automate 80-90% of design work**: A foundation model generating fully editable, parametric CAD geometry from text prompts, coming to Fusion and Forma.
- **SOLIDWORKS gets AI companions LEO and MARIE**: Interactive AI collaborators embedded in everyday mechanical design workflows via the 3DEXPERIENCE platform.
- **AI + real-time engines reshape architectural visualization**: Unreal Engine and AI rendering pipelines are converging to enable instant photorealistic walkthroughs from CAD models.

## Detailed Updates

### Autodesk Neural CAD — Text-to-Parametric Models
Autodesk's most disruptive development is Neural CAD, a category of 3D generative AI foundation models coming to Fusion and Forma products. The company says it could automate "80 to 90% of what designers typically do." Unlike earlier generative design tools that produced optimization outputs requiring reinterpretation, Neural CAD generates fully editable, parametric models from text prompts that engineers can immediately refine. The technology builds on Project Bernini research.

### SOLIDWORKS Virtual Companions
Dassault Systèmes introduced LEO and MARIE, two AI companions for SOLIDWORKS Design 2026. Designed for interactive collaboration through the 3DEXPERIENCE platform, they assist with drawing creation, detailing, and assembly modeling. SOLIDWORKS 2026 normalizes generative AI within everyday mechanical design, embedding AI directly into the tools designers already use.

### AI + Real-Time Engines for Architecture
A growing trend in April 2026 is the convergence of AI and real-time rendering engines (particularly Unreal Engine) for architectural 3D visualization. AI-driven rendering pipelines now produce photorealistic walkthroughs from CAD models in near-real-time, dramatically reducing the turnaround for client presentations and design reviews.

### Autodesk Fusion Automated Drawings
Fusion's Automated Drawings feature combines templates, heuristics, and AI to generate drawing sets for assemblies. It uses AI to classify standard fasteners and exclude them automatically — streamlining one of the most tedious parts of the engineering documentation process.

### Backflip AI and Adam AI — Emerging Tools
Backflip AI, which emerged from stealth in early 2025, turns 3D scanner data into fully parametric CAD models. Adam AI focuses on rapid prototyping, generating print-ready 3D models from text prompts within minutes. Both represent a new wave of specialized AI-CAD startups targeting specific workflow pain points.

### AI-Driven Constraint Solving
Modern CAD platforms are integrating AI-driven constraint solving that automatically resolves complex geometric relationships. This "invisible AI" approach enhances existing workflows without requiring designers to learn new tools, reducing time spent on manual constraint management.

### Gartner: AI-Assisted Modeling Becomes Standard
According to Gartner, by 2026 a majority of digital design workflows will include some level of AI-assisted modeling. The three AI capabilities becoming standard across all major CAD platforms: intelligent auto-completion of design intent, AI-powered simulation recommendations, and natural language interaction for design modifications.

### Generative AI Meets Manufacturing Processes
A new Springer Nature paper examines how large language models can enhance end-to-end engineering design-to-manufacturing workflows, bridging the gap between design optimization and production feasibility through AI-driven process planning.
