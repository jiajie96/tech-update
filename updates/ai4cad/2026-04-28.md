# AI for CAD Weekly Update — April 28, 2026

## Highlights

- Autodesk unveils Neural CAD, a generative AI foundation model targeting 80-90% automation of routine design tasks across Fusion and Forma.
- Solidworks 2026 embeds AI directly into drawing creation, detailing, and assembly modeling workflows.
- Siemens ships a conversational Copilot inside NX CAD, built in partnership with Microsoft.
- NASA demonstrates generative design pipelines that go from brainstorm to finished part in 36 hours.
- Text-to-3D tools reach a tipping point — usable models from plain English descriptions in seconds to minutes.

## Detailed Updates

### Autodesk Neural CAD

Autodesk introduced Neural CAD, a family of 3D generative AI foundation models integrated into Fusion and Forma. The system is trained on professional design data and reasons at both the geometry level and the systems/industrial process level. Designers can drive it through natural language, sketching, or reference imagery, and the output is first-class CAD geometry — not mesh approximations. Autodesk claims Neural CAD can automate 80-90% of what designers typically do, positioning it as a productivity multiplier rather than a replacement. If that figure holds up in practice, the implications for design cycle times are significant.

### Solidworks 2026

Dassault Systemes released Solidworks 2026 with AI capabilities woven into core mechanical design workflows. Drawing creation, detailing, and assembly modeling all benefit from embedded intelligence that handles repetitive steps and suggests completions. The message is clear: generative AI is no longer a separate feature or plugin — it is becoming a normalized part of everyday CAD work. For teams already on the Solidworks platform, the upgrade path is straightforward.

### Siemens Copilot in NX

Siemens, in collaboration with Microsoft, launched a conversational Copilot integrated into Designcenter NX CAD. Engineers can ask questions about their current design, examine material properties, and explore alternative design options through natural dialogue. While the user iterates, Copilot runs compliance checks in the background. This tight loop between exploration and validation could compress the review cycle considerably, especially for teams operating under strict regulatory requirements.

### NASA Generative Design

NASA has been applying generative design methods to accelerate hardware development. Their workflow moves from initial brainstorm to a physical part in hand within 36 hours — a timeline that would have been unthinkable with traditional methods. The results show 6-10x improvements across mass, stiffness, and overall design time. While NASA operates under unique constraints and budgets, the demonstrated gains suggest that generative design is ready for high-stakes engineering applications where performance-to-weight ratio matters most.

### Text-to-3D Reaches a Tipping Point

2026 is shaping up as the year text-to-3D becomes practical. Users can now describe a part or object in plain English and receive a usable 3D model in seconds. Adam AI generates print-ready 3D models from text prompts in minutes, while platforms like Tripo3D and Meshy AI continue to advance rapidly. The quality gap between AI-generated and manually modeled geometry is narrowing fast, though precision-critical applications still demand human refinement.

### ROI Reality Check

Despite the momentum, the return on investment picture is uneven. AI-powered design review — flagging errors, checking standards compliance, catching issues before fabrication — delivers measurable ROI today and is immediately deployable. Generative CAD, while promising, is not fully there yet for most organizations. Teams looking to adopt AI in their design workflows would be wise to start with review and validation tools, then layer in generative capabilities as the technology matures.
