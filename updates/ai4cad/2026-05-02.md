# AI for CAD Weekly Update — May 2, 2026

## Highlights

- **Siemens Showcases AI-Powered NX Updates at Hannover Messe**: Siemens unveiled new NX X Manufacturing products with an AI Copilot for CAM programming, immersive mixed-reality design, and lattice optimization for additive manufacturing.
- **AI Features Now Standard Across Major CAD Platforms**: Industry analysis confirms that AI-assisted capabilities have moved from optional add-ons to core features in Autodesk, Siemens, and SolidWorks products during 2026.
- **SOLIDWORKS Introduces Virtual Companions**: Two new AI assistants — LEO and MARIE — are coming to SOLIDWORKS Design for interactive collaboration through the 3DEXPERIENCE platform.

## Detailed Updates

### Siemens NX AI Copilot and Manufacturing Updates
At Hannover Messe 2026 (April 20–24), Siemens Digital Manufacturing showcased its latest NX updates in Hall 27. The highlights include an AI Copilot to aid CAM learning and programming, immersive engineering for mixed reality design and collaboration, and new Design for Manufacture capabilities. Siemens also introduced NX X Manufacturing CAM products focused on manufacturing workflows without requiring full CAD overhead. By year-end, NX X Manufacturing users will gain access to an AI assistant for rapid part programming.

### AI-Driven Lattice and Build Optimization for 3D Printing
Siemens expanded its additive manufacturing capabilities within NX. Lattice Structure Optimization now uses Simcenter 3D simulation to derive optimal lattice structures in a single environment. Part Orientation Optimization brings automated nesting of parts within a machine build area, combined with cloud-based orientation optimization to minimize thermal distortion. These features address a key bottleneck in metal additive manufacturing where build orientation dramatically affects part quality.

### SOLIDWORKS Virtual Companions LEO and MARIE
Dassault Systèmes announced two new Virtual Companions for SOLIDWORKS Design in 2026. LEO and MARIE are AI assistants designed for interactive collaboration through the 3DEXPERIENCE platform, helping designers navigate complex workflows and make informed design decisions. The assistants represent the broader trend of embedding conversational AI directly into CAD environments rather than offering it as a separate tool.

### Three AI Features Coming to Every CAD Program
An Engineering.com analysis identified three AI capabilities becoming standard across all major CAD platforms in 2026: intelligent constraint suggestion during sketching, automated design-for-manufacturability checks, and AI-powered search across design libraries. These features reflect a shift from AI as a premium add-on to a baseline expectation in professional design tools, driven by competitive pressure as each vendor races to match rivals.

### Generative Design Maturation
The generative design market continues to mature, with tools from Autodesk, Siemens, and newer entrants like Leo AI moving beyond topology optimization to encompass full parametric model generation. Current-generation tools can produce CAD-native geometry (not just mesh approximations), making the output directly usable in downstream manufacturing workflows. The key differentiator among vendors is increasingly how well AI-generated designs integrate with existing PDM/PLM systems.

### AI-Powered Open Source CAD Gains Traction
Market analysis from Energent.ai highlights growing interest in AI-powered open source CAD software, with several tools now offering AI-assisted features that were previously exclusive to commercial suites. This trend is particularly relevant for startups and educational institutions seeking generative design capabilities without enterprise licensing costs.
